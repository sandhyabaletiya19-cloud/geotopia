📋 Overview of Asia

📍 Location

Asia is the largest continent, located primarily in the Eastern and Northern Hemispheres. It is bounded by the Arctic Ocean to the north, Pacific Ocean to the east, Indian Ocean to the south, and Europe & Africa to the west.

🌐 Geographic Significance

Asia covers about 30% of Earth's total land area and houses about 60% of the world's population. It contains the world's highest mountain (Mt. Everest), largest lake (Caspian Sea), and deepest lake (Baikal).

🏛️ Cultural Heritage

Birthplace of major world religions (Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, Christianity) and ancient civilizations (Indus Valley, Mesopotamia, Chinese). Asia is known as the "Cradle of Civilization."

🏔️ Physical Features of Asia

Major Mountain Ranges

🏔️

Himalayas

World's highest mountain range with Mt. Everest (8,849m). Extends 2,400 km across 5 countries.

🏔️

Karakoram Range

Home to K2 (8,611m), the world's second highest peak. Contains world's largest glaciers outside poles.

🏔️

Hindu Kush

800 km mountain range across Afghanistan and Pakistan. Highest peak: Tirich Mir (7,708m).

🏔️

Kunlun Mountains

One of the longest mountain chains in Asia, extending 3,000 km across China.

🏔️

Tian Shan

Major mountain range in Central Asia. Highest peak: Jengish Chokusu (7,439m).

🏔️

Ural Mountains

Forms natural boundary between Europe and Asia. Length: 2,500 km.

⛏️ Natural Resources of Asia

🛢️ Oil & Natural Gas

Middle East holds 50%+ of world's proven oil reserves. Major producers: Saudi Arabia, Iran, Iraq, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar.

⚫ Coal

China & India are world's largest coal producers. Major coalfields in Shanxi (China), Jharkhand (India), Indonesia.

🔩 Iron Ore

China is world's largest iron ore producer. India's Chotanagpur Plateau is rich in iron deposits.

💎 Rare Earth Minerals

China controls 60% of world's rare earth production. Essential for electronics, renewable energy.
