Quick Facts

Fascinating records and statistics about protected areas worldwide

Largest Protected Area

Northeast Greenland National Park

Greenland, Denmark
Covering 972,000 km² — larger than most countries. Home to musk oxen, polar bears, Arctic foxes, and walruses. Established in 1974, expanded in 1988.
Oldest Protected Area

Bogd Khan Uul

Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia
Protected since 1778 — nearly 250 years of conservation. Sacred mountain revered by Mongolian rulers. Predates Yellowstone by nearly a century.
Most Biodiverse

Madidi National Park

La Paz, Bolivia
Contains 11% of world's bird species (over 1,100) in just 19,000 km². From Andean peaks to Amazon jungle. Possibly Earth's most biodiverse place.
First Modern National Park

Yellowstone National Park

Wyoming, USA
Established March 1, 1872. Set the template for national parks worldwide. Features geysers, hot springs, and the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48 states.

What is a Protected Area?

Understanding the concept from simple to scientific perspectives

🌳
Simple Definition

"Special outdoor places where nature is kept safe from harm — like a home for animals and plants where they can live without being disturbed by humans."

Kid-Friendly Explanation
🔬
Scientific Definition (IUCN)

"A clearly defined geographical space, recognised, dedicated and managed, through legal or other effective means, to achieve the long-term conservation of nature with associated ecosystem services and cultural values."

International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), 2008
4

IUCN Protected Area Categories

The international standard for classifying conservation areas

Ia

Strict Nature Reserve

Highest protection level

Areas set aside for scientific research and monitoring. Human visitation, use, and impacts are strictly controlled. Primary purpose is scientific study.

Examples
Wrangel Island (Russia) Aldabra Atoll (Seychelles) Greenland NE (Denmark) Białowieża (Poland)
Ib

Wilderness Area

Unmodified natural areas

Large unmodified or slightly modified areas retaining natural character. No permanent human habitation. Protected to preserve natural condition.

Examples
Gates of the Arctic (USA) Namib-Naukluft (Namibia) Tasmanian Wilderness (Australia) Fiordland (New Zealand)
II

National Park

Ecosystem protection & recreation

Large natural or near-natural areas protecting large-scale ecological processes with characteristic species and ecosystems. Provides foundation for environmentally and culturally compatible spiritual, scientific, educational, and recreational opportunities.

Examples
Yellowstone (USA) Serengeti (Tanzania) Galápagos (Ecuador) Kaziranga (India)
III

Natural Monument

Specific natural features

Set aside to protect a specific natural monument — landform, sea mount, submarine cavern, geological feature, or living feature like an ancient grove.

Examples
Devils Tower (USA) Giant's Causeway (UK) Iguazu Falls (Argentina) Victoria Falls (Zimbabwe)
IV

Habitat/Species Management Area

Wildlife sanctuaries

Aim to protect particular species or habitats. Management reflects this priority. Many need regular, active interventions to address species or habitat requirements.

Examples
Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary (India) Ascension Island (UK) Mkomazi Game Reserve (Tanzania)
V

Protected Landscape/Seascape

Human-nature interaction

Areas where interaction of people and nature over time has produced distinctive character with significant ecological, biological, cultural, and scenic value.

Examples
Lake District (UK) Cinque Terre (Italy) Ngorongoro Conservation (Tanzania)
VI

Protected Area with Sustainable Use

Managed resource areas

Conserve ecosystems and habitats, together with associated cultural values and traditional natural resource management systems. Generally large, mostly in natural condition, with sustainable use of natural resources as a main aim.

Examples
Amazon Extractive Reserves (Brazil) Great Barrier Reef (Australia) Laponia (Sweden)

Historical Timeline

The evolution of protected areas from ancient times to present

1778

First Recorded Protection

Bogd Khan Mountain in Mongolia becomes the first recorded protected natural area, preserved as a sacred site by the Mongolian rulers.

1872

Yellowstone Established

President Ulysses S. Grant signs the Yellowstone National Park Protection Act, creating the world's first modern national park.

1936

India's First National Park

Hailey National Park (now Jim Corbett) established as India's first national park, protecting the Bengal tiger and its habitat.

1948

IUCN Founded

International Union for Conservation of Nature established, becoming the global authority on the status of nature and conservation measures.

1960

IUCN Categories Created

First version of IUCN protected area categories established, providing a global framework for classifying conservation areas.

1971

Ramsar Convention

The Ramsar Convention on Wetlands signed, creating the first modern global intergovernmental environmental agreement.

1992

Rio Earth Summit

Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD) adopted at the Rio Earth Summit, providing framework for global biodiversity conservation.

2010

Aichi Biodiversity Targets

Target 11 set: protect 17% of terrestrial and 10% of marine areas by 2020. Became the global benchmark for conservation efforts.

2022

30x30 Goal Announced

Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework sets target to protect 30% of land and ocean by 2030, doubling current protection levels.
