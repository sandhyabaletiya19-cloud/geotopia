Understanding

What is UNESCO Natural Heritage?

From simple explanations to scientific definitions

Simple Definition

Special places in nature that are so amazing and important that the whole world agrees to protect them forever — like nature's museums where Earth keeps its greatest treasures!

— Explained for everyone

Scientific Definition

Natural heritage sites are areas of Outstanding Universal Value (OUV) that contain superlative natural phenomena, represent significant ongoing ecological and biological processes, or contain important natural habitats for in-situ conservation of biological diversity.

— UNESCO World Heritage Convention, 1972

Selection Criteria for Natural Sites

vii

Natural Phenomena

Contain superlative natural phenomena or areas of exceptional natural beauty and aesthetic importance.

viii

Geological Processes

Outstanding examples representing major stages of Earth's history, including the record of life and significant geological processes.

ix

Ecological Processes

Outstanding examples of significant ongoing ecological and biological processes in evolution and development of ecosystems.

x

Biodiversity

Contain the most important natural habitats for in-situ conservation of biological diversity, including threatened species of Outstanding Universal Value.

Origins

Formation & Development

How the World Heritage Convention came to protect Earth's natural treasures

1948

IUCN Founded

The International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) is established, creating the foundation for international nature conservation efforts.

1965

White House Conference

The idea of combining cultural and natural conservation under one international convention is proposed at the White House Conference in Washington, D.C.

1971

MAB Programme Launched

UNESCO's Man and the Biosphere (MAB) Programme is launched, establishing the concept of Biosphere Reserves.

1972

Convention Adopted

The World Heritage Convention is adopted by UNESCO's General Conference on November 16, 1972 — the founding document for protecting natural and cultural heritage.

1978

First Sites Inscribed

The first 12 sites are inscribed on the World Heritage List, including the Galápagos Islands and Yellowstone National Park.

2015

Global Geoparks Established

UNESCO formally establishes the International Geoscience and Geoparks Programme (IGGP), recognizing Global Geoparks as a new designation.
