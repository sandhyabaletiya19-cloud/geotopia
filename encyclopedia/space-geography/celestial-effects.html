🌍 Factors Affecting Insolation

• Angle of Sun's rays — Low angle = more atmosphere to penetrate, wider spread

• Transparency of atmosphere — Clouds, dust, water vapor absorb/reflect

• Day length — Longer days = more total energy received

• Earth's distance from Sun — Perihelion (Jan) vs Aphelion (Jul)

• Altitude — Less atmosphere above = more intense radiation

Earth's Heat Budget

Of every 100 units of solar radiation reaching Earth's atmosphere:

35 Units reflected back (Albedo)
14 Absorbed by atmosphere & clouds
51 Absorbed by Earth's surface
35+14 = 49 re-radiated back to space

⚖️ Earth maintains a heat balance — incoming solar radiation = outgoing terrestrial radiation. This balance keeps average temperature stable at ~15°C.

☀️ Solar Constant = 1,361 W/m² — energy received per unit area perpendicular to Sun's rays at Earth's mean distance.

Act 02

Eclipses

When celestial bodies align to perform the universe's most dramatic light show

☀️ Solar Eclipse
🌑 Lunar Eclipse
🔭
Total Solar

Moon fully covers Sun. Corona visible. Totality lasts up to 7 min 32 sec. Path width ~100-160 km.

💍
Annular Solar

Moon too far away — too small to fully cover Sun. "Ring of fire" effect visible around Moon.

🌑
Total Lunar

Earth's shadow fully covers Moon. Moon turns blood-red due to Earth's atmosphere bending red light.

🌓
Partial Eclipse

Only part of Sun/Moon enters shadow. Partial solar = crescent Sun. Partial lunar = dark bite from Moon.

🌑 Umbra

The darkest, innermost part of a shadow. Total eclipse occurs in the umbra. No direct sunlight penetrates here.

🌗 Penumbra

The lighter, outer shadow zone. Partial eclipse occurs here. Some sunlight still reaches through.

🔄 Saros Cycle

Eclipses repeat in a cycle of 18 years, 11 days, 8 hours — the Saros cycle. After one Saros, the Sun, Earth, and Moon return to nearly the same relative geometry, repeating the eclipse pattern.

Act 05

Solar Activity

Our star breathes — every 11 years it erupts in a cycle of magnetic fury

Solar Cycle (~11 Years) — Sunspot Number

We are currently in Solar Cycle 25 (2019–~2030). Solar maximum expected ~2025.

⚡

The Carrington Event — 1859

The most powerful geomagnetic storm ever recorded. Telegraph systems worldwide failed — operators received electric shocks, paper caught fire. Auroras visible as far south as Cuba and Hawaii. If it happened today, it would cost $2–20 trillion in damage to satellites, power grids, and global infrastructure.

🌑
Sunspots

Cooler (~3,500°C), darker regions on the Sun's surface caused by intense magnetic field activity. More sunspots = solar maximum. Cycle repeats every ~11 years.

🔥
Solar Flares

Sudden, intense bursts of energy from Sun's surface. Release X-rays and energetic particles. Reach Earth in 8 minutes at the speed of light. Disrupt radio communications.

💨
Coronal Mass Ejection (CME)

Massive bubbles of plasma and magnetic field hurled into space. Take 1–3 days to reach Earth. Trigger geomagnetic storms, auroras, and can knock out satellites.

🌬️
Solar Wind

Continuous stream of charged particles (mostly electrons & protons) flowing outward from Sun at 400–800 km/s. Earth's magnetosphere deflects most of it.

Act 07

Milankovitch Cycles

Three cosmic rhythms that orchestrate Ice Ages over thousands of years

🔵 ~100,000 yrs Eccentricity

Earth's orbital shape shifts between more circular and more elliptical. At maximum eccentricity, difference between perihelion and aphelion is greatest, affecting seasonal contrast.

🌀 ~41,000 yrs Obliquity (Axial Tilt)

Earth's tilt cycles between 22.1° and 24.5°. Greater tilt = more extreme seasons. Less tilt = milder seasons. Currently at 23.5° and decreasing.

🌍 ~26,000 yrs Precession

Earth's axis wobbles like a spinning top. This changes which pole faces the Sun during perihelion. In ~13,000 years, the Northern Hemisphere summer will occur during perihelion.

Act 08

Celestial Phenomena

Supermoons, blood moons, meteor showers, comets — the cosmos never stops performing

🌕
Supermoon

When a full moon coincides with perigee (Moon's closest point to Earth). Moon appears up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than a typical full moon.

Up to 356,500 km away
🩸
Blood Moon

Total lunar eclipse where the Moon turns deep red/orange. Earth's atmosphere filters out blue light and bends red light onto the Moon — like all Earth's sunrises/sunsets projected on the Moon.

Rayleigh Scattering
💙
Blue Moon

The second full moon in a calendar month — occurs roughly every 2.7 years. In the older definition, it's the 3rd full moon in a season with 4 full moons.

Once in a Blue Moon
⭐
Meteor Showers

Earth passing through debris trails left by comets. Perseids (Aug), Leonids (Nov), Geminids (Dec) — 50 to 150 meteors per hour at peak.

Comet debris trails
☄️
Comets

Icy bodies from Kuiper Belt/Oort Cloud. Develop glowing coma and tail as they approach the Sun (solar wind pushes tail away from Sun). Halley's Comet: 75-year period.

Dirty snowballs
🌟
Zodiacal Light

Faint, triangular glow visible on the horizon after sunset or before sunrise. Caused by sunlight scattering off interplanetary dust particles in the ecliptic plane.

Best in February/March
🪐
Planetary Conjunction

When two or more planets appear very close to each other in the sky (as seen from Earth). The Great Conjunction of Jupiter and Saturn in Dec 2020 was 0.1° apart.

Apparent proximity
☀️
Transit (Venus/Mercury)

When Venus or Mercury crosses the face of the Sun as seen from Earth. Venus transits occur in pairs 8 years apart, separated by 100+ years. Last: 2004 & 2012.

Next Venus: 2117
🌈
Earthshine

When sunlight reflects off Earth onto the dark side of a crescent Moon, making it faintly visible. Leonardo da Vinci first correctly explained this phenomenon.

Da Vinci's Glow
