☁️ Earth Systems

Atmosphere & Ocean

The fluid envelopes that make Earth habitable. Atmospheric layers, clouds, pressure systems, currents, and circulation patterns.

🌍 Atmospheric Layers

🛸 Exosphere (500-10,000 km)

Transition to space. Satellites orbit here. Molecules can escape to space. Contains hydrogen and helium.

🌌 Thermosphere (80-500 km)

Extremely hot (1500°C) but feels cold (few molecules). Aurora occurs here. ISS orbits at 400 km. Radio waves reflect off ionosphere.

☄️ Mesosphere (50-80 km)

Coldest layer (-90°C at top). Meteors burn up here. Noctilucent clouds form. Too high for aircraft, too low for spacecraft.

🛩️ Stratosphere (12-50 km)

Contains ozone layer (UV protection). Temperature increases with height. Jets fly in lower stratosphere. Stable, no weather.

⛅ Troposphere (0-12 km)

Where we live. Contains 80% of atmosphere mass. All weather happens here. Temperature decreases ~6.5°C per km. Extends to 18km at equator.

☁️ Cloud Classification

High Clouds (6-12 km)

Made of ice crystals

Cirrus ☁️

Wispy, hair-like. Fair weather but may indicate front approaching.

Cirrostratus 🌤️

Thin sheet, creates halo around sun/moon. Rain in 12-24 hours.

Cirrocumulus 🐑

"Mackerel sky" - small ripples. Usually fair weather.

Middle Clouds (2-6 km)

Water droplets, some ice

Altostratus ☁️

Gray sheet covering sky. Sun appears "watery." Rain likely coming.

Altocumulus 🌥️

Gray/white patches. "Mare's tails." May indicate thunderstorms.

Low Clouds (0-2 km)

Water droplets

Stratus 🌫️

Gray blanket, overcast. Drizzle possible. Fog is stratus at surface.

Stratocumulus 🌥️

Lumpy gray/white. Most common cloud type. Usually dry.

Nimbostratus 🌧️

Dark gray, continuous rain/snow. Hides sun completely.

Cumulus ☁️

"Cauliflower" puffy clouds. Fair weather cumulus = nice day. Can grow into cumulonimbus. Flat base, rounded top.

Cumulonimbus ⛈️

The thunderstorm cloud. Can reach 12+ km. Produces lightning, heavy rain, hail, tornadoes. Anvil top from hitting stratosphere.

🌊 Ocean Circulation

Surface Currents (Wind-Driven)

Top 400m, driven by prevailing winds. Form circular gyres in each ocean basin.

Major Warm Currents

  • Gulf Stream: Warms Western Europe (+10°C)
  • Kuroshio: Warms Japan
  • Brazil Current: Warms SE South America
  • Agulhas: Warms SE Africa

Major Cold Currents

  • California: Cools US West Coast + fog
  • Humboldt: Creates Atacama Desert
  • Benguela: Creates Namib Desert
  • Canary: Cools NW Africa

Thermohaline Circulation

"The Great Ocean Conveyor Belt" - deep circulation driven by temperature and salinity differences. Takes 1000 years for full cycle.

⚠️ Climate Risk

Freshwater from Greenland ice melt is slowing this circulation. Already 15% weaker. Collapse would severely cool Europe.

🔄 ENSO: El Niño & La Niña

🔴 El Niño

Warm water moves east across Pacific. Trade winds weaken. Typically raises global temperature.

Effects

  • South America: Heavy rains, floods
  • Australia: Drought, fires
  • Indonesia: Drought
  • Africa: Drought in south/east
  • US: Wet South, warm North
  • Fewer Atlantic hurricanes

🔵 La Niña

Cold water dominates eastern Pacific. Trade winds strengthen. Often follows El Niño.

Effects

  • South America: Drought
  • Australia: Heavy rains, floods
  • Indonesia: Heavy rains
  • Africa: Floods in east
  • US: Dry South, cold North
  • More Atlantic hurricanes

⚪ Neutral

Normal Pacific conditions. Neither warming nor cooling anomaly.
