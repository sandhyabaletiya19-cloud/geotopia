📖 How Köppen Classification Works

First Letter: Main Group

  • A = Tropical (Tcoldest >18°C)
  • B = Dry (Evaporation > Precipitation)
  • C = Temperate (Tcoldest 0-18°C)
  • D = Continental (Tcoldest <0°C)
  • E = Polar (Twarmest <10°C)

Second Letter: Precipitation

  • W = Desert (very low precip)
  • S = Steppe/Semi-arid
  • f = No dry season
  • s = Dry summer
  • w = Dry winter
  • m = Monsoon
  • T = Tundra | F = Ice cap

Third Letter: Temperature

  • h = Hot (mean >18°C)
  • k = Cold (mean <18°C)
  • a = Hot summer (>22°C)
  • b = Warm summer (10-22°C)
  • c = Cool summer (1-3 mo >10°C)
  • d = Very cold winter (<-38°C)

🔬 Why It Exists Here

  • Equatorial location (0-10° latitude)
  • Constant solar radiation year-round
  • ITCZ convergence = constant convective rain
  • High sea surface temperatures

🌿 Characteristics

  • ✓ Hot year-round (25-28°C)
  • ✓ No dry season (>60mm every month)
  • ✓ 2000-4000mm annual rainfall
  • ✓ Daily afternoon thunderstorms
  • ✓ High humidity (80%+)

🌳 Natural Environment

  • 🌲 Tropical rainforest biome
  • 🦜 Highest biodiversity on Earth
  • 🌿 Multi-layered canopy structure
  • 🐒 Primates, jaguars, tree frogs

⚠️ Climate Change 2100

  • 📈 +2-3°C temperature increase
  • 🌧️ More intense but less frequent rain
  • 🔥 Increased drought risk
  • ⬆️ Potential shift to Aw (savanna)

BWh 🏜️ Hot Desert 12.5% 20-45°C summer, 10-25°C winter <250mm/year Dubai, Phoenix, Cairo, Alice Springs

  • ✓ Huge diurnal range (20°C+)
  • ✓ <250mm annual precipitation
  • ✓ Clear skies 300+ days/year
  • ✓ Low humidity (<30%)
