Extreme Dynamics

Temperature & Wind Phenomena

From scorching heat domes to the biting polar vortex. Explore the mechanisms behind extreme temperature shifts and unique local wind systems that shape our planet's weather patterns.

🔥

Heat Phenomena

Mechanisms of extreme warming and heat transport.

High Pressure

Heat Dome

An area of high pressure that parks over a region, trapping sinking hot air like a lid on a pot.

Duration: Days to Weeks Severity: Critical

⚙️ Mechanism

  • High pressure system causes air to sink (subsidence).
  • Sinking air compresses and warms adiabatically.
  • The high pressure dome blocks cooler air/winds.
  • Surface air stagnates, accumulating heat.

⚠️ Impacts

  • Nighttime temperatures remain high (no relief).
  • High humidity can make it lethal (Heat Index).
  • Power grid stress due to A/C demand.
  • Drought intensification.
Anthropogenic

Urban Heat Island (UHI)

Cities become significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas due to human activity and materials.

Diff: 1-7°C higher Peak: Nighttime

⚙️ Causes

  • Asphalt and concrete absorb heat (high thermal mass).
  • Lack of vegetation (evapotranspiration is reduced).
  • Waste heat from vehicles and HVAC systems.
  • Tall buildings trap heat (canyon effect).

🏙️ Mitigation

  • Cool roofs (reflective surfaces).
  • Green roofs and vertical gardens.
  • Urban tree planting.
  • Permeable pavements.
Transport

Saharan Air Layer (SAL)

A mass of very dry, dusty air originating from the Sahara Desert that moves across the Atlantic.

Altitude: 1.5-6 km Route: To Americas

⚙️ Characteristics

  • Extreme dry air (suppresses hurricane formation).
  • Dust particles create hazy skies ("Red Saharan Dust").
  • Warm layer of air sits atop cooler Atlantic air.
  • Creates colorful sunsets in the Caribbean/USA.

🌍 Effects

  • Inhibits tropical cyclone development.
  • Can degrade air quality in the Americas.
  • Dust fertilizes the Amazon rainforest.
  • Sudden temperature rise upon arrival.
Rare

Heat Burst

A rare meteorological phenomenon where dry air plunges to the ground, causing temperatures to spike rapidly at night.

Speed: Sudden Condition: Night

⚙️ Mechanism

  • Rain evaporates into dry air aloft.
  • Evaporation cools the air parcel.
  • Dense, cooled air plummets to the ground.
  • Compression heating warms it drastically (5-20°C rise).

⚠️ Experience

  • Can happen at 2 AM - 4 AM.
  • Rapid temp rise (e.g., 20°C → 35°C in minutes).
  • Extreme drying effect.
  • Usually associated with decaying thunderstorms.

Cold Phenomena

Extreme cooling events and cryospheric dynamics.

Stratospheric

Polar Vortex

A large area of low pressure and cold air surrounding the poles. When it weakens, cold air spills south.

Altitude: Stratosphere Season: Winter

⚙️ The Split

  • Normally, a strong jet stream keeps cold air locked north.
  • Sudden Stratospheric Warming (SSW) weakens the vortex.
  • The vortex can split or stretch.
  • Extremely cold air plunges into mid-latitudes.

🥶 Real World

  • 2019 "Polar Vortex" brought -40°F to US Midwest.
  • Causes major travel disruptions.
  • Energy demand spikes (gas/heating).
  • Risk of frostbite in minutes.
Local

Lake-Effect Snow

Narrow bands of intense snowfall downwind of large lakes, occurring when cold air moves over warmer water.

Intensity: Extreme Location: Downwind

⚙️ Mechanism

  • Cold air absorbs heat and moisture from lake.
  • Warmer, moist air rises (convection).
  • Friction at shore forces air upward further.
  • Clouds dump snow in narrow bands (10-15km wide).

🏔️ "Snowbelts"

  • Buffalo, NY (Lake Erie).
  • Cleveland, OH (Lake Erie).
  • Grand Rapids, MI (Lake Michigan).
  • Can drop 2-5 inches of snow per hour.
Explosive

Bomb Cyclone

A mid-latitude cyclone that intensifies rapidly, dropping pressure by 24mb in 24 hours.

Pressure Drop: >24mb/24h Wind: Hurricane Force

⚙️ Ingredients

  • Contrast between warm ocean and cold continental air.
  • Jet stream provides energy.
  • Explosive cyclogenesis.
  • Combines hurricane winds with snow.

🚨 Hazards

  • Blizzard conditions (whiteouts).
  • Coastal flooding (storm surge + high tide).
  • Widespread power outages.
  • Difficult to forecast precise path.
Acoustic

Frost Quake

A cracking noise and shaking feeling caused by sudden freezing of water-saturated soil.

Condition: Flash Freeze Sensation: Explosion

⚙️ Mechanism

  • Ground is saturated with water (after rain/snow melt).
  • Temp drops rapidly (e.g., 0°C to -20°C).
  • Water expands as it freezes underground.
  • Soil/rock cracks under stress.

💥 Experience

  • Sound like a gunshot or thunder.
  • Vibrations felt on the ground.
  • Mistaken for structural damage or earthquakes.
  • No earthquake signature on seismographs.
Optical

Diamond Dust

Ground-level cloud composed of tiny ice crystals. Occurs in very cold air near the poles.

Temp: Below -15°C Visual: Sparkling

⚙️ Formation

  • Air temperature drops below -15°C (often -40°C).
  • Air is very clear and still.
  • Water vapor sublimates directly into ice crystals.
  • Crystals hang suspended near the ground.

✨ Effects

  • Creates halos, sun pillars, and sundogs.
  • Makes the ground look like it is covered in glitter.
  • Can reduce visibility.
  • Common in Antarctica and Arctic.

Local Wind Systems

Named winds shaped by topography and geography.

Snow Eater

Chinook Wind

A warm, dry wind blowing down the eastern slopes of the Rockies. Can raise temps 20°C in minutes.

Effect: Rapid Warming Region: North America

⚙️ Mechanism

  • Moist air rises windward side (orographic lift).
  • Clouds form and precipitate (rain/snow).
  • Dry air descends leeward side.
  • Air compresses and heats up (Adiabatic heating).

🏔️ Impact

  • "Snow Eater": Sublimates snow rapidly.
  • Can cause rapid snowmelt flooding.
  • Triggers headaches for some people.
  • Creates Archimedes lenticular clouds.
Fire Weather

Santa Ana Winds

Strong, extremely dry downslope winds that originate inland and affect coastal Southern California.

Speed: >100 km/h Danger: Extreme

⚙️ Formation

  • High pressure over Great Basin (Utah/Nevada).
  • Air flows downslope toward coast.
  • Mountain passes compress wind (Venturi effect).
  • Heats up and dries out further.

🔥 Impact

  • Critical fire weather conditions.
  • Fires spread rapidly (wind-driven).
  • Humidity can drop below 5%.
  • Reduces visibility with dust.
Cold North

Mistral

A strong, cold, northwesterly wind that blows through southern France into the Mediterranean.

Speed: Up to 100 km/h Region: Mediterranean

⚙️ Path

  • Cold air builds over Central Plateau of France.
  • Funneled down Rhône Valley.
  • Accelerates due to valley topography.
  • Exits into Mediterranean sea.

🌊 Effects

  • Brings clear skies but cold temperatures.
  • Can last for days ("Mistral blows for 3, 6, or 9 days").
  • Dries out vegetation.
  • Can make sea crossing rough.
Dusty South

Sirocco

A hot, dry wind blowing from North Africa across the Mediterranean, often carrying sand.

Temp: >30°C Visibility: Poor

⚙️ Origin

  • Drawn from Algerian/Saharan deserts.
  • Crosses Mediterranean Sea.
  • Picks up moisture, making it humid yet hot.
  • Often arrives as a reddish dust storm.

🌍 Reach

  • Italy (often called "Scirocco").
  • Greece (Etesian winds).
  • Spain (Levante).
  • Can reach as far north as France.
Sandstorm

Haboob

An intense dust storm carried on an atmospheric gravity current, usually caused by thunderstorm downdrafts.

Height: 1000m+ Speed: >80 km/h

⚙️ Formation

  • Downburst from a severe thunderstorm.
  • Cold air hits the ground and spreads out.
  • Lofting dust and sand into the air.
  • Forms a rolling wall of dust.

⚠️ Safety

  • Zero visibility instantly.
  • Respiratory issues.
  • Static electricity storms.
  • Pull off road immediately if driving.
Gravity Driven

Katabatic Wind

A drainage wind that carries high-density air from a higher elevation down a slope under gravity.

Direction: Downslope Example: Bora

⚙️ Types

  • Warm Katabatic: Chinook/Foehn (drying air).
  • Cold Katabatic: Mistral/Bora (very cold).
  • Antarctica's strong winds are Katabatic.
  • Nighttime land breezes are mild Katabatic.

🌍 Locations

  • Antarctica (driest continent due to them).
  • Yugoslav coast (Bora).
  • California (Santa Ana - technically katabatic).
  • Alpine valleys.
