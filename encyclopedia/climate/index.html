📚 Basics First

Weather vs Climate

⛈️

Weather

→ Short-term conditions

→ Day-to-day changes

→ "What's it like outside?"

→ Measured in hours/days

Example: "It's raining today"
VS
🌍

Climate

→ Long-term average

→ Pattern over 30+ years

→ "What's the region usually like?"

→ Measured in decades

Example: "Mumbai has tropical monsoon climate"
💡

Easy Memory Trick: "Climate is what you expect, weather is what you get!"

🌡️ Köppen Classification

Major Climate Types

Based on temperature and precipitation patterns

🌴

Tropical (A)

🌡️ > 18°C all year 🌧️ High rainfall
Af - Rainforest Am - Monsoon Aw - Savanna

📍 Amazon, Congo, Kerala
🏜️

Dry/Arid (B)

🌡️ Extreme temps 🌧️ < 250mm rain
BWh - Hot desert BWk - Cold desert BSh - Hot semi-arid

📍 Sahara, Thar, Gobi
🍂

Temperate (C)

🌡️ -3°C to 18°C (coldest) 🌧️ Moderate rainfall
Cfa - Humid subtropical Cfb - Oceanic Csa - Mediterranean

📍 California, Italy, Sydney
❄️

Continental (D)

🌡️ < -3°C (coldest) 🌧️ Variable
Dfa - Hot summer Dfb - Warm summer Dfc - Subarctic

📍 Moscow, Chicago, Manchuria
🧊

Polar (E)

🌡️ < 10°C (warmest) 🌧️ Very low precip
ET - Tundra EF - Ice cap

📍 Antarctica, Greenland, Arctic
⛰️

Highland (H)

🌡️ Varies with altitude 🌧️ Orographic rainfall
Multiple zones Based on elevation

📍 Himalayas, Andes, Alps
