📊 Evidence of Climate Change

🧊

Ice Core Records

800,000 years of data

Air bubbles trapped in Antarctic ice reveal past CO₂ levels. Current 423 ppm is 50% higher than any point in 800,000 years.

Ice age CO₂: ~180 ppm
Interglacial CO₂: ~280 ppm
Today: 423 ppm
🏔️

Glacier Retreat

Visible proof worldwide

Glaciers shrinking on every continent. Some have lost 50%+ of mass since 1900. Glacier National Park had 150 glaciers in 1850; now has 25.

Rate of Loss

  • Alps: -50% since 1850
  • Himalaya: 1.5m/year thinning
  • Kilimanjaro: 85% gone since 1912
🌊

Ocean Heat Content

90% of warming goes here

Oceans absorb 90%+ of excess heat. Top 2000m has warmed significantly. This drives sea level rise (thermal expansion), intensifies hurricanes.

+345
Zettajoules absorbed since 1955
(= 1.5 Hiroshima bombs/second)

Extreme Weather

Increasing frequency
  • Heat waves: 5x more likely than 1900
  • Heavy precipitation: +7%/°C warming
  • Cat 4-5 hurricanes: +25% since 1980
  • Wildfire season: 78 days longer (US West)
🌸

Seasonal Shifts

Spring comes earlier
  • Spring: 2-3 days earlier per decade
  • Cherry blossoms: 10 days earlier (Japan)
  • Bird migration: 2 days earlier/decade
  • Growing season: 2 weeks longer since 1900
🧪

Ocean Acidification

"Climate change's evil twin"

CO₂ dissolves in seawater, forming carbonic acid. Ocean pH has dropped 0.1 (30% more acidic) since 1800.

Pre-industrial pH: 8.2
Current pH: 8.1
2100 (high emissions): 7.7-7.8

🏭 What's Causing It?

Greenhouse Gas Sources

Attribution Science

Scientists can distinguish natural vs human-caused warming using "fingerprints" - patterns unique to different causes.

Human-caused warming >100%
Why >100%? Natural factors would have caused slight cooling. Human emissions overcome this AND add warming.

Key Evidence

  • Stratosphere cooling while surface warms
  • Nights warming faster than days
  • Winter warming faster than summer
  • Carbon isotope signature matches fossil fuels
