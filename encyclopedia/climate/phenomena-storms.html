🔬 Formation Requirements

  • Sea surface temp: >26.5°C to 50m depth
  • Wind shear: Low vertical shear (<20 knots)
  • Distance from equator: >5° latitude (Coriolis needed)
  • Pre-existing disturbance: Tropical wave, old front
  • Mid-level humidity: High (moist air needed)
  • Instability: Unstable atmosphere for convection

🌀 Life Cycle

  1. Tropical Disturbance: Unorganized thunderstorms
  2. Tropical Depression: Organized, <39 mph
  3. Tropical Storm: Named, 39-73 mph
  4. Hurricane: Eye forms, >74 mph
  5. Major Hurricane: Cat 3+, >111 mph

🌏 Most Active Basin on Earth

The Northwest Pacific produces more tropical cyclones than any other basin - about 26 per year, or 30% of the global total. The vast warm water pool and favorable atmospheric conditions create ideal formation conditions year-round.

🎌 Countries Affected

  • 🇵🇭 Philippines: ~20 typhoons/year (most struck nation)
  • 🇯🇵 Japan: ~10 typhoons/year
  • 🇹🇼 Taiwan: ~4-5/year
  • 🇨🇳 China: ~7-8/year (coastal)
  • 🇻🇳 Vietnam: ~4-5/year
  • 🇰🇷 South Korea: ~2-3/year

📊 Classification (JMA Scale)

  • Tropical Depression: <34 knots
  • Tropical Storm: 34-47 knots
  • Severe Tropical Storm: 48-63 knots
  • Typhoon: 64-84 knots
  • Very Strong Typhoon: 85-104 knots
  • Violent Typhoon: >105 knots

⚠️ Strongest Typhoons Ever

🌀 Typhoon Haiyan (2013)
315 km/h winds at landfall (Philippines)
6,300+ deaths, $2.9 billion damage
🌀 Typhoon Tip (1979)
Largest ever: 2,220 km diameter
Lowest pressure: 870 mb
🌀 Super Typhoon Goni (2020)
315 km/h at landfall (strongest landfall ever)
🌀 Typhoon Nancy (1961)
Estimated 345 km/h (before satellite era)

🔥 Why So Powerful?

  • Warm Pacific pool (29-30°C)
  • Low wind shear environment
  • Long fetch over open water
  • High ocean heat content (deep warm layer)

🔬 Formation Process

  1. Wind shear: Winds at different heights create horizontal rotation
  2. Updraft: Strong updraft tilts rotation vertical
  3. Mesocyclone: Rotating updraft in supercell
  4. RFD: Rear-flank downdraft wraps around
  5. Tornado: Rotation tightens, funnel descends

🗺️ Tornado Alley

Central US where cold Canadian air, warm Gulf moisture, and dry Rocky Mountain air collide. Texas to Nebraska is the core, but "Dixie Alley" (SE US) is increasingly active.

  • Texas: ~140/year (#1)
  • Kansas: ~100/year
  • Oklahoma: ~60/year
  • Nebraska: ~55/year

🧊 Hail Formation

Strong updrafts carry water droplets above freezing level. Ice grows in layers as stone cycles up and down. Falls when too heavy for updraft.

📏 Hail Size Records

Largest diameter: 20.3 cm (SD, 2010)
Heaviest: 1.02 kg (Bangladesh, 1986)
US damage/year: $10-15 billion

🛡️ 30-30 Lightning Rule

If thunder follows lightning in <30 seconds, seek shelter. Stay inside 30 minutes after last thunder.

⚠️ Bay of Bengal: Deadliest Waters

The funnel-shaped Bay of Bengal concentrates storm surge, making it the deadliest region for tropical cyclones. Bangladesh's low-lying delta is extremely vulnerable.

📅 Two Seasons

  • Pre-monsoon (Apr-May): Before SW monsoon arrives
  • Post-monsoon (Oct-Nov): After monsoon retreats
  • Monsoon itself (Jun-Sep) suppresses cyclone formation due to wind shear

🔬 What Makes It "Super"

The key feature is a persistent, rotating updraft called a mesocyclone. This rotation allows the storm to continuously regenerate, lasting hours instead of minutes.

📋 Requirements

  • Wind shear: Strong speed AND directional shear
  • Instability: High CAPE values (>2000 J/kg)
  • Moisture: High dewpoints (60°F+)
  • Lift: Front, dryline, or outflow boundary

🏗️ Supercell Structure

  • Anvil: Ice-crystal top spreads downwind
  • Overshooting top: Punches through tropopause
  • Mesocyclone: Rotating updraft core
  • Wall cloud: Lowered rotating base
  • FFD: Forward-flank downdraft (rain)
  • RFD: Rear-flank downdraft (clear slot)
  • Tail cloud: Feeds into wall cloud

⚠️ Supercell Types

Classic Supercell
Separate updraft/downdraft, visible structure, moderate rain
HP (High Precipitation)
Heavy rain wraps around, poor visibility, flash flood risk
LP (Low Precipitation)
Minimal rain, stunning structure visible, large hail

🌪️ Tornado Production

~25% of supercells produce tornadoes. The strongest tornadoes (EF4-EF5) come almost exclusively from supercells.

🔬 What Is a Derecho?

Spanish for "straight" - a derecho is a widespread, long-lived windstorm associated with a fast-moving band of severe thunderstorms. Unlike tornadoes, the winds are straight-line, not rotating.

📋 Official Criteria

  • Wind damage path >400 km (240 mi) long
  • Wind gusts >93 km/h (58 mph) along most of path
  • At least 3 reports of >119 km/h (74 mph) gusts
  • No more than 3-hour gap in reports

🔄 Formation

Forms from bow echoes - when thunderstorm outflow creates a bowing line of storms. The cold outflow accelerates the line forward, creating hurricane-force winds along the leading edge.

⚠️ Notable Derechos

  • Winds up to 225 km/h (140 mph)
  • $11.5 billion damage (costliest thunderstorm ever)

📋 Official NWS Criteria

  • Sustained winds OR gusts >56 km/h (35 mph)
  • Visibility <400m (¼ mile) from falling/blowing snow
  • Conditions persist for >3 hours
  • Note: Snowfall amount is NOT a criterion

🔬 Formation Types

  • Synoptic blizzard: From major low-pressure systems
  • Lake-effect: Cold air over warm lakes (Great Lakes)
  • Ground blizzard: Wind blowing existing snow (no new snow)

🌡️ Wind Chill Danger

-40°F with 30 mph wind = feels like -70°F. Frostbite can occur in under 5 minutes at these conditions.

🔬 How Freezing Rain Forms

❄️ SNOW starts high up
↓
🌡️ WARM LAYER melts snow to rain
↓
❄️ THIN cold layer (doesn't refreeze)
↓
💧 Supercooled rain FREEZES ON CONTACT

❄️ vs ❄️ Sleet

Sleet: Rain refreezes in air → falls as ice pellets (bounces)
Freezing rain: Rain stays liquid, freezes when hitting cold surface (glazes)

📏 Ice Accumulation Impacts

  • Trace: Slippery surfaces
  • 3-6 mm: Hazardous driving
  • 6-12 mm: Power outages likely, tree damage
  • 12-25 mm: Catastrophic, widespread damage
  • >25 mm: Disaster, grid collapse

⚠️ Most Dangerous Winter Event

Ice storms cause more damage and longer-lasting impacts than blizzards. Power can be out for weeks, and ice-laden trees continue falling for days.

🔬 Why "Nor'easter"?

Named for the strong northeast winds that blow in from the ocean ahead of the storm. As the low-pressure system moves up the coast, counter-clockwise circulation brings these characteristic winds to coastal areas.

📋 Formation Recipe

  • Gulf Stream: Warm water provides moisture & energy
  • Cold continental air: Strong temperature contrast
  • Jet stream: Upper-level support for intensification
  • Coastal front: Boundary for development

🌡️ Rain vs Snow Line

The position of the storm track determines who gets snow vs rain. Storms closer to coast bring warmer air inland = rain. Storms tracking farther offshore keep cold air locked in = more snow.

🔬 Formation

"Haboob" comes from Arabic "هَبوب" meaning "blowing furiously." Forms when thunderstorm downdrafts hit the ground and spread outward, picking up loose sand and dust.

📋 Process

  1. Thunderstorm forms over desert
  2. Cold downdraft hits surface
  3. Outflow spreads in all directions
  4. Loose sediment lifted into air
  5. Wall of dust advances ahead of storm

🗺️ Where They Occur

  • 🇸🇩 Sudan (origin of term)
  • 🇺🇸 Arizona, New Mexico, Texas
  • 🇦🇺 Australian Outback
  • 🇸🇦 Arabian Peninsula
  • 🇮🇳 Rajasthan, India
  • 🌍 Sahel region

⚠️ Hazards

  • Zero visibility: Multi-car pileups
  • Respiratory: Asthma attacks, dust inhalation
  • Aviation: Engines can fail
  • Property: Abrasive damage
  • Disease: Valley fever (coccidioidomycosis)

💀 Notable Events

1930s Dust Bowl (US)
Not haboobs but massive dust storms
Drought + poor farming = ecological disaster
"Black Sunday" 1935: 300,000+ tons of topsoil lost
2011 Phoenix Haboob
Wall 1,500m high, 160km wide
Visible from space

🛡️ Safety

  • "Pull aside, stay alive" - Pull off road
  • Turn off lights (cars follow lights into pileups)
  • Keep windows closed
  • Cover nose and mouth

📋 Two Types

Fair-Weather Waterspout
  • Forms from surface up (not from cloud)
  • Light winds (EF0 equivalent)
  • Develops under building cumulus
  • Dissipates when hitting land
  • Most common type (95%)
Tornadic Waterspout
  • Actual tornado that forms over water
  • OR land tornado moving over water
  • Can be violent (EF2+)
  • Associated with severe thunderstorms
  • Dangerous if moving onshore

🗺️ Waterspout Hotspots

  • 🇺🇸 Florida Keys: 400-500/year (world capital)
  • 🇺🇸 Great Lakes: Fall season, cold air over warm water
  • 🇮🇹 Adriatic Sea: Common in summer
  • 🇦🇺 Australia: Tropical waters
  • 🇬🇷 Mediterranean: Hundreds annually

🔬 Formation Conditions

  • Light winds and little shear
  • Warm water (>26°C optimal)
  • High humidity
  • Developing cumulus clouds
  • Convergence zones (wind shifts)

⚠️ Boater Safety

  • Move at right angles to spout's path
  • Do NOT try to navigate through
  • Watch for dark spots on water (forming)
  • Can capsize small boats

🇮🇳 South Asian Monsoon

The most powerful monsoon system on Earth. Land heats faster than ocean, creating low pressure that draws moist ocean air inland. The Himalayas block this moisture, causing intense rainfall.

📅 Timing

  • Onset: June 1 (Kerala), advances north
  • Peak: July-August
  • Retreat: September, south to north
  • NE Monsoon: Oct-Dec (Tamil Nadu)

💧 Rainfall Records

  • Mawsynram, India: 11,871 mm/year (wettest)
  • Cherrapunji: 26,461 mm in one year (1861)
  • Mumbai: 944 mm in 24 hours (2005)

🌍 Other Monsoons

🌍 West African
Jun-Sep, brings Sahel rains
Failure = famine (1970s, 1980s)
🌏 East Asian
Mei-Yu (China), Baiu (Japan), Changma (Korea)
June rainy season, then typhoons
🌎 North American
Arizona/Mexico, Jul-Sep
Afternoon thunderstorms daily
🦘 Australian
Dec-Mar (wet season)
Darwin: 90% of rain in summer

⚠️ Monsoon Hazards

  • Catastrophic flooding (Mumbai 2005: 1,000+ deaths)
  • Landslides in hill regions
  • Disease outbreaks (cholera, dengue)
  • Crop destruction from floods
  • Infrastructure collapse

📈 Climate Change Impact

  • Monsoon becoming more erratic
  • Delayed onset in recent decades
  • More intense but shorter bursts
  • "Active" and "break" periods more extreme
  • Models predict +10-25% rainfall intensity

🌾 Importance

India's economy still depends on monsoon. A weak monsoon can reduce GDP by 2-5%. "Finance minister is the monsoon."

⚠️ Storm Safety Quick Guide

🌀 Hurricane Safety

  • Know your evacuation zone
  • Board up windows
  • 3-day supply of water (1 gal/person/day)
  • Fill car with gas before storm
  • Evacuate if ordered
  • Stay away from windows during storm
  • Don't go outside in "eye" - worst is coming
  • After: watch for downed lines, flooding

🌪️ Tornado Safety

  • Go to basement or interior room
  • Lowest floor, away from windows
  • Get under sturdy furniture
  • Cover head with arms
  • If in car: abandon for sturdy building or ditch
  • Never try to outrun in a vehicle
  • Mobile homes: leave for sturdy building
  • Never shelter under overpass

⚡ Lightning Safety

  • 30-30 Rule: If thunder within 30 sec of flash, go inside. Stay 30 min after last thunder
  • Get off elevated areas
  • Stay away from isolated trees
  • Get out of water immediately
  • Avoid metal objects
  • Car is safe (metal frame, not rubber tires)
  • Crouch low if caught outside (don't lie flat)

❄️ Winter Storm Safety

  • Stay indoors if possible
  • Dress in layers
  • Keep emergency kit in car
  • If stranded: run engine 10 min/hr, crack window
  • Avoid overexertion shoveling
  • Watch for signs of frostbite/hypothermia

🌊 Flood Safety

  • Turn Around, Don't Drown
  • Never drive through floodwater
  • 6 inches can knock you down
  • 2 feet can float a car
  • Move to higher ground
  • Don't walk through moving water
  • Stay off bridges over fast water

📱 Emergency Contacts

  • US: 911 (Emergency)
  • NOAA Weather Radio: 162.4-162.55 MHz
  • NWS Alerts: weather.gov
  • UK: 999 / Met Office
  • EU: 112
