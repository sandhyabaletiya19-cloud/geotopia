Panch Prayag (पंच प्रयाग) - 5 Sacred Confluences

Five holy confluences in Uttarakhand where tributaries merge to form the Ganga (Alaknanda stream)

Prayag Name Rivers Meeting Location Elevation Significance
1. Vishnuprayag Alaknanda + Dhauliganga Chamoli, Uttarakhand 1,372m Vishnu appeared here in Narasimha avatar
2. Nandaprayag Alaknanda + Nandakini Chamoli, Uttarakhand 1,358m Named after King Nanda
3. Karnaprayag Alaknanda + Pindar Chamoli, Uttarakhand 1,451m Karna (Mahabharata) worshipped Sun god here
4. Rudraprayag Alaknanda + Mandakini Rudraprayag, Uttarakhand 895m Rudra (Shiva) performed tandava here
5. Devprayag Alaknanda + Bhagirathi Tehri Garhwal, Uttarakhand 830m Named "GANGA" after this point

Note: After Devprayag, the river is officially called Ganga. Before this, the main source stream is Bhagirathi (from Gangotri) which merges with Alaknanda (which has already absorbed 4 tributaries via the first 4 prayags).

Brahmaputra River System (ब्रह्मपुत्र नदी तंत्र)

The Mighty Brahmaputra - Son of Brahma | Complete coverage including all tributaries

Brahmaputra (ब्रह्मपुत्र) Tibet: Yarlung Tsangpo | Bangladesh: Jamuna

2,900 km Total
916 km in India
Braided River
Origin & Geography
Origin: Angsi Glacier (Chemayungdung Glacier), Kailash Range, Tibet
Coordinates: 30.55°N, 82.0°E
Elevation: 5,210m at source
Total Length: 2,900 km (Tibet: 1,625 km; India: 916 km; Bangladesh: 359 km)
Basin Area: 712,035 km² (India: 194,413 km²)
Course: Tibet (eastward as Yarlung Tsangpo) → Great Bend → India (Arunachal) → Assam (westward) → Bangladesh (southward as Jamuna) → Bay of Bengal
Indian States: Arunachal Pradesh Assam
Major Cities: Pasighat, Dibrugarh, Tezpur, Guwahati, Dhubri
Outflow: Bay of Bengal (joins Ganga in Bangladesh to form Ganga-Brahmaputra Delta)
Unique Characteristics
  • Trans-Himalayan: Originates north of Himalayas, crosses them via Great Bend
  • Great Bend: 180° turn around Namcha Barwa (7,782m) - world's deepest gorge (Yarlung Tsangpo Canyon - 5,590m deep)
  • Braided River: Multiple channels; forms river islands
  • Majuli: World's largest river island (352 km²) in Brahmaputra
  • Discharge: 19,800 m³/s (2nd highest in India after Ganga)
  • Floods: Annual devastating floods in Assam
  • Course Change: Shifts channels frequently; high silt load
Name Changes
In Tibet: Yarlung Tsangpo (ཡར་ཀླུངས་གཙང་པོ་) - "River from highest peak"
At Entry (Arunachal): Siang / Dihang
In Assam: Brahmaputra (ব্ৰহ্মপুত্ৰ) - "Son of Brahma"
In Bangladesh: Jamuna (যমুনা) - different from Indian Yamuna
After joining Ganga: Padma (পদ্মা)
Final name (after joining Meghna): Meghna (মেঘনা) - empties into Bay of Bengal
Major Projects
  • Subansiri Lower (Arunachal-Assam) - 2,000 MW (under construction; India's largest hydro project)
  • Ranganadi (Arunachal) - 405 MW
  • Kameng (Arunachal) - 600 MW
  • Dhola-Sadiya Bridge (Assam-Arunachal) - 9.15 km; India's longest bridge over Brahmaputra
  • Bogibeel Bridge (Assam) - 4.94 km; rail-road bridge
Environmental Challenges
Annual Floods: Affects 31+ districts in Assam; damages crops, infrastructure annually
Erosion: Bank erosion displaces thousands; 8,000 km² land lost in last 40 years
Silt Load: ~735 million tonnes/year (one of world's highest)
China Factor: Concerns over Chinese dams in Tibet affecting flow

West Flowing Rivers (Arabian Sea)

1. Narmada (नर्मदा)
Rewa | Rift Valley River | Sacred Narmada Maiya
1,312 km (5th longest in India)
Origin & Course
Origin: Amarkantak, Anuppur district, Madhya Pradesh (1,057m)
States (3): Madhya Pradesh Maharashtra Gujarat
Basin Area: 98,796 km²
Outflow: Arabian Sea (Gulf of Khambhat, near Bharuch, Gujarat)
Estuary: 25 km wide estuary (not delta - strong tidal currents prevent delta formation)
Unique Geology
  • Rift Valley: Flows through tectonic rift between Vindhya & Satpura ranges
  • Fault Line River: Follows geological fault
  • Oldest River: Among oldest in India (geological age)
  • No Delta: Only forms estuary due to hard rock & tidal action
Major Cities
  • Jabalpur (MP) - Marble Rocks, Dhuandhar Falls
  • Hoshangabad (MP)
  • Omkareshwar (MP) - Jyotirlinga
  • Maheshwar (MP) - Ahilya Fort
  • Bharuch (Gujarat)
Tributaries
All from MP/Maharashtra: Hiran, Tawa, Orsang, Banjar, Sher, Shakkar, Dudhi, Tawa, Ganjal, Chhota Tawa, Kolar, Man, Uri, Hatni, Orsang
Major: Tawa (largest tributary - 172 km)
Major Dams
  • Sardar Sarovar Dam (Gujarat) - 1,450 MW; 163m high; Controversial project
  • Indira Sagar Dam (MP) - 1,000 MW; Largest reservoir in India by volume
  • Omkareshwar Dam (MP) - 520 MW
  • Maheshwar Dam (MP) - 400 MW (under construction)
Religious Significance
  • One of 7 sacred rivers (Sapta Sindhu)
  • Mentioned in Rigveda
  • Narmada Parikrama - 2,600 km circumambulation (takes 3 years!)
  • 2 Jyotirlingas on banks - Omkareshwar, Maheshwar
  • Every pebble considered Shiva Linga
Famous Features
Dhuandhar Falls: 10m high; Marble Rocks gorge, Jabalpur (smoke-like spray)
Marble Rocks: 3 km gorge with white & colored marble cliffs (100 ft high)

Origin & Course
