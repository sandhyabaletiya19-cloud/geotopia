📌 Key Geographic Facts

India lies entirely in the Northern Hemisphere (above Equator) and Eastern Hemisphere (east of Prime Meridian). The country spans approximately 3,214 km from North to South and 2,933 km from East to West. Despite this large longitudinal extent, India maintains a single time zone (IST = UTC+5:30) based on the 82°30'E meridian.

☀️ Tropic of Cancer & Standard Meridian

☀️ Tropic of Cancer (23°26'N)

Passes through 8 Indian states, dividing India into tropical (south) and subtropical (north) zones. This imaginary line marks the northernmost point where the Sun can appear directly overhead.

States Crossed (West → East):
  1. Gujarat Jasdan, near Rajkot district
  2. Rajasthan Banswara, Dungarpur, Kalinjarh
  3. Madhya Pradesh Ratlam, Ujjain, Shajapur, Bhopal, Vidisha, Sagar, Damoh, Katni
  4. Chhattisgarh Surajpur, Koriya, Balrampur
  5. Jharkhand Latehar, Lohardaga, Ranchi
  6. West Bengal Purulia, Bankura, Burdwan, Nadia
  7. Tripura Udaipur, Amarpur, Belonia
  8. Mizoram Champhai, near Myanmar border
☀️ Geographic Significance:
  • Sun directly overhead on June 21 (Summer Solstice)
  • Divides India into Tropical (south) & Subtropical (north)
  • Affects climate, vegetation & agricultural patterns
  • Nearly divides India into two equal halves
  • Maximum sunlight duration varies across this line
🕐 Standard Meridian (82°30'E)

Determines Indian Standard Time (IST = UTC+5:30) for the entire nation. Located centrally to minimize time differences between eastern and western extremes.

Primary Reference Point: Mirzapur, Uttar Pradesh (Clock Tower)
States/Cities Crossed: UP (Prayagraj, Mirzapur), MP (Katni, Rewa), CG (Bilaspur), Odisha (Balangir), AP (Machilipatnam)
Time Offset: UTC + 5 hours 30 minutes
Calculation: 82.5° × 4 min/degree = 330 min = 5h 30m
🤔 Why 82°30'E?

India's longitudinal extent spans ~29° (68°7'E to 97°25'E), creating a ~2 hour time difference between extremes. The 82°30'E meridian was chosen as it passes through the center of the country, minimizing discrepancy between local and standard time. The historic reference is the Mirzapur Clock Tower in Uttar Pradesh.

🏔️ Physiographic Divisions of India भारत के भौतिक विभाग

Based on relief, geological structure, drainage patterns, and formation history, India is divided into 5 major physiographic divisions. Each division possesses unique characteristics that influence climate, soil types, vegetation, drainage patterns, and human settlement patterns. These divisions range from the youngest fold mountains (Himalayas) to one of the world's oldest landmasses (Peninsular Plateau).

1

The Himalayan Mountains

हिमालय पर्वत श्रृंखला

Length: 2,500 km
Width: 150-400 km
Area: ~5,00,000 km²
Peak: K2 (8,611m)

Key Features:

  • ✓ Youngest fold mountains (~50 million years)
  • ✓ Four parallel ranges: Trans, Himadri, Himachal, Shiwaliks
  • ✓ Source of perennial rivers (Ganga, Indus, Brahmaputra)
  • ✓ Biodiversity hotspot with unique flora & fauna
  • ✓ Climate barrier protecting India from cold waves
Explore Himalayas →
2

The Northern Plains

उत्तरी मैदान

Length: 2,400 km (E-W)
Width: 150-300 km
Area: ~7,00,000 km²
Elevation: 50-300m

Key Features:

  • ✓ Formed by alluvial deposits from Himalayan rivers
  • ✓ World's most fertile agricultural region
  • ✓ Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra river systems
  • ✓ Highest population density in India
  • ✓ Four relief zones: Bhabar, Terai, Bhangar, Khadar
Explore Plains →
3

The Peninsular Plateau

प्रायद्वीपीय पठार

Area: ~16,00,000 km²
Elevation: 300-1,200m
Age: 3.6 billion years
Peak: Anaimudi (2,695m)

Key Features:

  • ✓ Part of ancient Gondwanaland supercontinent
  • ✓ Rich mineral deposits (iron, coal, manganese)
  • ✓ Deccan Trap - volcanic basalt region
  • ✓ Western & Eastern Ghats border edges
  • ✓ Seasonal (rain-fed) peninsular rivers
Explore Plateau →
4

The Coastal Plains

तटीय मैदान

West Coast: ~1,400 km
East Coast: ~2,500 km
Coastline: 7,516 km
Width: 10-130 km

Key Features:

  • ✓ Western: Narrow (Konkan, Malabar) - submerged coast
  • ✓ Eastern: Wider (Coromandel, Circars) - emergent coast
  • ✓ Major ports & harbors (natural & artificial)
  • ✓ Fishing, coconut, rice cultivation
  • ✓ Lagoons, backwaters, deltas
Explore Coasts →
5

The Indian Islands

भारतीय द्वीप समूह

Bay of Bengal: 572 islands
Arabian Sea: 36 islands
Total: ~1,382 islands
Area: ~8,300 km²

Key Features:

  • ✓ Andaman & Nicobar: Volcanic/tectonic origin
  • ✓ Lakshadweep: Coral atolls
  • ✓ Unique endemic biodiversity
  • ✓ Strategic maritime & defense importance
  • ✓ Tropical rainforests, coral reefs, mangroves
