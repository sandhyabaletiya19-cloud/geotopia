☀️ Tropic of Cancer (23°30'N)

Passes through 8 Indian states, dividing India into tropical and subtropical zones.

States Crossed (West to East):
  1. Gujarat Jasdan (near Rajkot)
  2. Rajasthan Kalinjarh, Banswara
  3. Madhya Pradesh Shajapur, Ujjain, Ratlam
  4. Chhattisgarh Sonhat, Surajpur
  5. Jharkhand Lohardaga, Ranchi
  6. West Bengal Bankura, Purulia
  7. Tripura Udaipur, Amarpur
  8. Mizoram Champhai
Geographic Impact:
  • ✓ Sun directly overhead on June 21 (Summer Solstice)
  • ✓ Divides India: North = Subtropical, South = Tropical
  • ✓ Affects climate, vegetation, and agricultural patterns
🕐 Standard Meridian (82°30'E)

Determines Indian Standard Time (IST = UTC+5:30) for the entire nation.

Passes Through: Mirzapur, Uttar Pradesh
Also Passes: Naini (Prayagraj), Katni (MP), Balangir (Odisha)
Offset from Greenwich: +5 hours 30 minutes
Rationale: Centrally located, 5.5 hours ahead of GMT
Why 82°30'E?

The longitudinal extent of India spans nearly 30° (68°7'E to 97°25'E), which would translate to about 2 hours time difference between eastern and western extremes. To maintain uniformity, the central meridian passing through Mirzapur was chosen as the Standard Meridian.

🌍 Equator (0°)

Does NOT pass through India.

Distance from Southernmost Point: ≈ 876 km South of Indira Point
Nearest Country on Equator: Maldives (crosses at Gaafu Dhaalu Atoll)

🏔️ Physiographic Divisions of India भारत के भौतिक विभाग

Based on relief, structure, geological history, and drainage, India is divided into 5 major physiographic divisions. Each division has distinct characteristics that influence climate, soil, vegetation, and human settlement patterns.

1

The Himalayan Mountains

हिमालय पर्वत

Length: 2,500 km
Width: 240-320 km
Area: ~612,000 km²
Highest Peak: K2 (8,611m)

Key Features:

  • ✓ Youngest fold mountains (50 million years)
  • ✓ Three parallel ranges: Himadri, Himachal, Shiwaliks
  • ✓ Source of perennial rivers
  • ✓ Rich biodiversity hotspot
  • ✓ Climate barrier (blocks cold winds)
Explore Himalayas →
2

The Northern Plains

उत्तरी मैदान

Length: 2,400 km (E-W)
Width: 240-320 km
Area: ~700,000 km²
Avg Elevation: 100-300m

Key Features:

  • ✓ Formed by alluvial deposits from Himalayan rivers
  • ✓ World's most fertile agricultural region
  • ✓ Ganga-Brahmaputra river systems
  • ✓ Highest population density
  • ✓ Three zones: Punjab, Ganga, Brahmaputra Plains
Explore Plains →
3

The Peninsular Plateau

प्रायद्वीपीय पठार

Area: ~1,600,000 km²
Avg Elevation: 600-900m
Age: Oldest (Pre-Cambrian)
Rock Type: Igneous & Metamorphic

Key Features:

  • ✓ Part of ancient Gondwanaland
  • ✓ Rich in minerals (iron ore, coal, manganese)
  • ✓ Deccan Trap (volcanic basalt)
  • ✓ Black soil (regur) region
  • ✓ Seasonal rivers (non-perennial)
Explore Plateau →
4

The Coastal Plains

तटीय मैदान

Western Coast: 1,400 km
Eastern Coast: 1,500 km
Width: 10-25 km (W), 80-100 km (E)
Total Coastline: 7,516 km

Key Features:

  • ✓ Western: Narrow, Konkan-Malabar coasts
  • ✓ Eastern: Wider, Coromandel-Northern Circars
  • ✓ Major ports and harbors
  • ✓ Fishing and coconut cultivation
  • ✓ Tropical climate, high rainfall (Western)
Explore Coasts →
5

The Indian Islands

भारतीय द्वीप समूह

Bay of Bengal: 572 islands
Arabian Sea: 36 islands
Total Islands: 1,382
Area: 8,249 km²

Key Features:

  • ✓ Andaman & Nicobar: Volcanic origin
  • ✓ Lakshadweep: Coral atolls
  • ✓ Unique endemic biodiversity
  • ✓ Strategic maritime importance
  • ✓ Tropical rainforests & coral reefs
