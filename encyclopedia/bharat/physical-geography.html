💡 Key Fact

India lies entirely in the Northern Hemisphere (north of the Equator) and entirely in the Eastern Hemisphere (east of the Prime Meridian). The Tropic of Cancer (23°30'N) passes almost through the middle of the country, dividing India into roughly two equal halves — the tropical south and the subtropical north.

Chapter 02

Shape & Size

India's inverted triangular shape and how it compares with other major nations in area

🔷 The Shape of India

India has the shape of an inverted triangle (also described as a quadrilateral when including the northern mountainous bulge). The broad base lies in the north along the Himalayan range, and the country tapers southward to the narrow point at Kanyakumari.

The shape is often compared to a rhombus or an irregular quadrilateral by geographers. The northern boundary is broadly east-west, while the southern peninsula forms a triangular projection into the Indian Ocean, flanked by the Arabian Sea on the west and the Bay of Bengal on the east.

Dimensions

  • North-South extent: 3,214 km (from Indira Col to Kanyakumari)
  • East-West extent: 2,933 km (from Gujarat's Rann of Kutch to Arunachal Pradesh)
  • Land frontier: 15,106.7 km
  • Coastline: 7,516.6 km (mainland + islands)
  • Total area: 3,287,263 km² (including PoK & Aksai Chin — integral parts of India)
🌍 India's Share

India accounts for 2.4% of the world's total land area, yet supports approximately 17.7% of the world's population — making it one of the most densely populated large nations on Earth.

Chapter 03

Latitudinal & Longitudinal Extent

Understanding the geographic implications of India's coordinate spread

📐 Latitudinal Extent

India extends from 8°4'N (Kanyakumari, mainland) to 37°6'N (Indira Col, Ladakh) — a total latitudinal span of approximately 29°. If we include the Andaman & Nicobar Islands (Indira Point at 6°45'N), the span increases to about 30°21'.

This extensive north-south spread means India experiences a wide range of climatic conditions — from tropical in the south to temperate and alpine in the extreme north. The Tropic of Cancer (23°30'N) divides India into two nearly equal halves:

  • Southern Half (below 23°30'N): Tropical zone — receives sun's rays more directly; warmer throughout the year
  • Northern Half (above 23°30'N): Subtropical zone — experiences distinct seasons and greater temperature variation

📐 Longitudinal Extent

India extends from 68°7'E (Ghuar Mota, Gujarat) to 97°25'E (Kibithu, Arunachal Pradesh) — a total longitudinal span of approximately 29°.

Despite the nearly equal degrees of latitude and longitude (~29° each), the north-south distance (3,214 km) exceeds the east-west distance (2,933 km) because lines of longitude converge toward the poles. At India's average latitude (~23°N), each degree of longitude covers about 102 km (vs 111 km per degree of latitude).

⏰ Time Difference

The longitudinal extent of ~29° means there is a time difference of approximately 2 hours between the easternmost and westernmost points of India. The sun rises about 2 hours earlier in Arunachal Pradesh than in Gujarat. To avoid confusion, India follows a single standard time: IST (Indian Standard Time) based on the 82°30'E meridian passing through Mirzapur, Uttar Pradesh.

Tropic of Cancer Passage

The Tropic of Cancer (23°30'N) passes through 8 states of India, dividing it into tropical and subtropical zones

The Tropic of Cancer at 23°30'N latitude is one of the five major circles of latitude on Earth. In India, it passes through 8 states from west to east. Areas south of this line receive more direct sunlight and have a tropical climate, while areas to the north experience a subtropical climate with greater seasonal variation.

Chapter 05

Standard Meridian (82°30'E)

Indian Standard Time (IST) and its significance

🕐 Indian Standard Time (IST)

India follows a single time zone based on the 82°30'E longitude, which passes through Mirzapur (near Allahabad/Prayagraj) in Uttar Pradesh. This was chosen because it falls roughly in the center of the country's longitudinal extent.

IST is UTC+5:30 (5 hours and 30 minutes ahead of Greenwich Mean Time). India does not observe daylight saving time (DST).

Why a Single Time Zone?

Although India's east-west span creates a natural ~2-hour time difference (between Arunachal Pradesh and Gujarat), a single time zone was adopted for:

  • Administrative simplicity — uniform governance and scheduling
  • Railway operations — single timetable for the vast rail network
  • Communication — no confusion in business and daily life
  • National unity — one nation, one time
🌅 Practical Impact

In Dong village (Arunachal Pradesh), the sun rises as early as 4:00 AM IST during summer, while in parts of Gujarat, sunrise occurs around 6:30 AM IST at the same time. The northeast has occasionally advocated for a separate "Chaibagaan Time" (Tea Garden Time), which is 1 hour ahead of IST, though this has not been officially adopted.

📊 Elevation Records of India

🏔️
Highest Point: K2 / Godwin-Austen (8,611m) in India's PoK region. In administered territory: Kangchenjunga (8,586m), Sikkim — 3rd highest in the world.
🌊
Lowest Point: Kuttanad, Kerala at -2.2m below sea level — one of the few places in India below sea level, famous for below-sea-level farming.
📏
Mean Elevation: India's average elevation is approximately 160m above sea level (much lower than the global average of 840m because of the vast Northern Plains).
🗻
Highest Plateau: The Ladakh Plateau at ~5,000m — one of the highest inhabited plateaus in the world, also called "cold desert."
🌿
Highest Peak (Peninsular India): Anamudi, Kerala at 2,695m in the Western Ghats — highest south of the Himalayas.
⛰️
Deepest Valley: The Brahmaputra gorge near Namcha Barwa (5,382m depth) — one of the deepest river gorges on Earth.

Detailed Extent Comparison

Understanding why N-S exceeds E-W despite equal degree spans

Parameter North-South East-West
Angular Extent ~29° latitude ~29° longitude
Linear Distance 3,214 km 2,933 km
Distance per Degree ~111 km (constant) ~102 km (at 23°N)
From Indira Col, Ladakh Ghuar Mota, Gujarat
To Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu Kibithu, Arunachal Pradesh
Key Implication Wide climatic diversity (tropical to alpine) ~2 hours sunrise time difference
Why Different? Meridians (longitude lines) converge toward the poles. At the equator, 1° longitude = 111 km, but at 23°N (India's avg), 1° ≈ 102 km. Hence, despite equal degree spans, N-S distance exceeds E-W.
