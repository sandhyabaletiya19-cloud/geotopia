Natural Disasters

👶 For a 5-Year-Old

Natural disasters are like Earth's temper tantrums! Sometimes our planet gets angry and shakes (earthquakes), cries really hard (floods), or throws hot rocks from mountains (volcanoes). They're nature's way of releasing energy, but they can be scary and dangerous for people.

🎈 Did You Know? The word "disaster" comes from Italian "disastro," meaning "bad star" - ancient people thought bad events happened when stars were in the wrong position!

🎓 Academic Definition

Natural disasters are catastrophic events resulting from natural processes of the Earth, causing significant damage to property, loss of life, and disruption of normal societal functions. They occur due to geological, meteorological, hydrological, climatological, and biological processes, often with minimal warning and devastating consequences.

Etymology: "Disaster" (Late 16th century) - from Italian disastro 'ill-starred event', from dis- (expressing negation) + astro 'star' (from Latin astrum).

🌱

Environmental Issues

👶 For a 5-Year-Old

Environmental issues are like when you don't clean your room and it gets messy! When people don't take care of Earth - cutting too many trees, making air dirty with smoke, or throwing trash in oceans - our planet gets "sick." Just like you need a clean room to be healthy, Earth needs to be clean too!

🎈 Did You Know? If we put all the plastic ever made into plastic bags, we could cover an area twice the size of India! That's a lot of mess! 🌏

🎓 Academic Definition

Environmental issues are harmful effects of human activities on the biophysical environment, including pollution, resource depletion, habitat destruction, biodiversity loss, and climate change. These issues arise from the complex interactions between human socioeconomic systems and natural ecosystems, often resulting in long-term ecological degradation and sustainability challenges.

Etymology: "Environment" (Early 17th century) - from French environner 'surround, enclose', from environ 'surroundings'.

🌋 Natural Disasters - Complete Encyclopedia

In-depth exploration of Earth's most powerful natural phenomena

1. 🏔️ Earthquakes

Simple Explanation

Imagine Earth's surface is like a giant jigsaw puzzle made of huge pieces called "tectonic plates." These pieces are always slowly moving, rubbing against each other. When they suddenly slip or bump, the ground shakes - that's an earthquake! It's like when you push two toy blocks together and one suddenly pops out.

📚 Scientific Definition

An earthquake is the result of a sudden release of stored energy in the Earth's crust that creates seismic waves. This energy release occurs along fault lines where tectonic plates meet, causing ground shaking, surface rupture, and in severe cases, liquefaction and tsunamis. Earthquakes are measured using the Richter scale (0-10) or Moment Magnitude Scale (Mw).

🔬 Formation Process

  • Plate Tectonics: Earth's lithosphere consists of 7 major and numerous minor tectonic plates floating on the asthenosphere
  • Stress Accumulation: Plates move at 2-15 cm/year, building stress at boundaries over decades or centuries
  • Elastic Rebound: When stress exceeds rock strength, sudden slip occurs along faults
  • Seismic Wave Propagation: Energy radiates as P-waves (primary, fastest), S-waves (secondary, slower), and surface waves (most destructive)
  • Aftershocks: Continued adjustment of crustal stress can cause secondary earthquakes for months or years

📊 Classification by Magnitude

  • Micro (< 2.0): Not felt, recorded by seismographs only - ~8,000 occur daily worldwide
  • Minor (2.0-3.9): Often felt, rarely cause damage - ~1,000 occur daily
  • Light (4.0-4.9): Noticeable shaking, minimal damage - ~49,000 annually
  • Moderate (5.0-5.9): Can damage poorly constructed buildings - ~6,200 annually
  • Strong (6.0-6.9): Destructive in populated areas up to 160 km radius - ~800 annually
  • Major (7.0-7.9): Serious damage over large areas - ~120 annually
  • Great (8.0-8.9): Catastrophic in areas several hundred kilometers across - ~15 annually
  • Extreme (9.0+): Devastating across 1,000+ km, triggers tsunamis - 1-2 per decade

🌍 Types by Tectonic Setting

  • Interplate Earthquakes: Occur at plate boundaries (convergent, divergent, transform) - 90% of all earthquakes
  • Intraplate Earthquakes: Within tectonic plates, often along ancient fault zones - less frequent but can be severe
  • Volcanic Earthquakes: Caused by magma movement, swarms indicate eruption potential
  • Induced Earthquakes: Human-caused via mining, reservoir filling, fracking, nuclear testing

⚠️ Effects & Hazards

  • Ground Shaking: Primary cause of damage, intensity varies with distance and local geology
  • Surface Rupture: Visible displacement along fault lines, can offset roads/structures by meters
  • Liquefaction: Saturated soil loses strength, acts like liquid - catastrophic for foundations
  • Landslides: Triggered on unstable slopes, can bury communities
  • Tsunamis: Submarine earthquakes (>6.5) can generate devastating ocean waves
  • Fires: Ruptured gas lines and electrical systems ignite urban fires
  • Economic Impact: 2011 Tōhoku earthquake cost $235 billion (most expensive disaster in history)

🌏 Most Earthquake-Prone Regions

  • Pacific Ring of Fire: 90% of world's earthquakes - Chile, Japan, Indonesia, Philippines, USA (Alaska, California)
  • Alpide Belt: Mediterranean to Himalayas - Italy, Greece, Turkey, Iran, India, Nepal
  • Mid-Atlantic Ridge: Underwater divergent boundary - Iceland experiences frequent activity
  • East African Rift: Continental rifting - Kenya, Ethiopia, Tanzania

📅 Historic Major Earthquakes

  • 1556 Shaanxi, China: Deadliest recorded - 830,000 deaths (magnitude ~8.0)
  • 1906 San Francisco, USA: 7.9 magnitude, $524M damage (1906 dollars), 3,000+ deaths
  • 1960 Valdivia, Chile: Largest recorded - 9.5 magnitude, entire Pacific Rim affected
  • 2004 Indian Ocean: 9.1-9.3 magnitude, triggered tsunami killing 230,000 across 14 countries
  • 2011 Tōhoku, Japan: 9.1 magnitude, 15,899 deaths, Fukushima nuclear disaster
  • 2015 Nepal: 7.8 magnitude, 9,000 deaths, destroyed UNESCO World Heritage Sites
🔬 Research Insight: Scientists estimate Earth experiences about 500,000 detectable earthquakes annually, with 100,000 felt by humans and only 100 causing damage. The energy released by a 9.0 earthquake equals approximately 32,000 nuclear bombs!

🌊 Tsunamis

Definition: Series of ocean waves with extremely long wavelengths (up to 200 km) caused by large-scale disturbances of ocean water.

Causes:

  • Submarine earthquakes (75%)
  • Volcanic eruptions (5%)
  • Underwater landslides (7%)
  • Meteorite impacts (rare)

Characteristics:

  • Travel 800 km/h in deep ocean
  • Slow to 50 km/h near coast
  • Wave height increases 10-30m
  • Multiple waves over hours

Most Affected: Pacific Rim countries (Japan, Indonesia, Chile, Philippines)

🌋 Volcanic Eruptions

Definition: Ejection of magma, ash, and gases from beneath Earth's crust through volcanic vents, fissures, or craters.

Types by Explosivity:

  • Effusive: Gentle lava flows (Hawaii)
  • Explosive: Violent ejections (Mt. St. Helens)
  • Phreatomagmatic: Magma-water interaction

Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI):

  • 0-2: Non-explosive to mild (daily)
  • 3-5: Moderate to very large (yearly)
  • 6-8: Colossal to mega-colossal (centuries apart)

Global Distribution: ~1,500 active volcanoes, 75% along Ring of Fire

🌊 Floods

Definition: Overflow of water onto normally dry land, most common natural disaster affecting 2+ billion people worldwide.

Types:

  • Fluvial: River overflow from rainfall
  • Pluvial: Surface water from extreme rain
  • Coastal: Storm surge, tsunamis
  • Flash floods: Sudden, within 6 hours
  • Glacial lake outburst: Dam failure

Causes: Heavy rainfall, snowmelt, dam failure, deforestation, urbanization

Impact: 44% of all disaster deaths (1995-2015)

🏜️ Droughts

Definition: Prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall leading to water shortage, classified by duration and severity.

Types:

  • Meteorological: Precipitation deficit
  • Agricultural: Soil moisture deficit
  • Hydrological: Surface/groundwater deficit
  • Socioeconomic: Supply-demand imbalance

Measurement: Palmer Drought Severity Index (PDSI), Standardized Precipitation Index (SPI)

Vulnerable Regions: Sub-Saharan Africa, Middle East, Central Asia, Southwest USA, Australia

🏔️ Landslides

Definition: Downward movement of rock, soil, or debris on slopes due to gravity, causing ~4,000 fatalities annually.

Types:

  • Falls: Free-falling rock/debris
  • Topples: Forward rotation
  • Slides: Movement along rupture surface
  • Flows: Liquefied material movement
  • Spreads: Lateral extension

Triggers: Rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, human excavation

Hotspots: Himalayas, Andes, Rockies, Alps, Japan, Philippines

❄️ Avalanches

Definition: Rapid flow of snow down mountainside, reaching speeds of 130 km/h, burying everything in path.

Types:

  • Slab avalanche: Cohesive plate (deadly)
  • Loose snow: Point release
  • Powder: Airborne snow cloud
  • Wet avalanche: Heavy, slow-moving

Danger Scale: 1 (Low) to 5 (Extreme)

Factors: Slope angle (30-45°), snowpack stability, weather, human trigger

Deaths: 150-250 annually worldwide

🌀 Cyclones/Hurricanes/Typhoons

Definition: Rotating low-pressure systems with organized thunderstorms; same phenomenon, different names by region.

Classification (Saffir-Simpson):

  • Category 1: 119-153 km/h winds
  • Category 2: 154-177 km/h
  • Category 3: 178-208 km/h (major)
  • Category 4: 209-251 km/h
  • Category 5: 252+ km/h (catastrophic)

Naming: Hurricane (Atlantic/E Pacific), Typhoon (NW Pacific), Cyclone (S Pacific/Indian Ocean)

Formation: Warm ocean (>26.5°C), low wind shear, Coriolis effect

🔥 Wildfires

Definition: Uncontrolled fire in wildland areas, burning vegetation and spreading rapidly under wind/drought conditions.

Types:

  • Surface fires: Ground vegetation
  • Crown fires: Tree canopies
  • Ground fires: Organic soil/peat

Causes: Lightning (45%), human activity (55% - arson, campfires, cigarettes)

Fire Triangle: Fuel + Oxygen + Heat = Fire

Hotspots: Australia, California, Mediterranean, Amazon, Siberia, Indonesia

Scale: 70,000-80,000 wildfires annually in USA alone

🌨️ Blizzards

Definition: Severe snowstorm with sustained winds ≥56 km/h, visibility <400m, lasting ≥3 hours.

Formation:

  • Cold air mass meets warm, moist air
  • Strong low-pressure system
  • Temperature near/below freezing

Types:

  • Ground blizzard: Wind-blown existing snow
  • Severe blizzard: Winds >72 km/h, temperature <-12°C

Hazards: Hypothermia, frostbite, whiteout conditions, infrastructure damage

Regions: Northern USA/Canada, Russia, Northern Europe, Antarctic

⚡ Thunderstorms & Lightning

Definition: Atmospheric disturbance with thunder, lightning, heavy rain, sometimes hail/tornadoes. ~16 million annually worldwide.

Types:

  • Single-cell: Brief, isolated
  • Multi-cell: Cluster, moderate severity
  • Supercell: Rotating, most severe

Lightning: 100 million volts, 30,000°C, kills 24,000 people/year globally

Formation: Unstable atmosphere + moisture + lifting mechanism

🌪️ Tornadoes

Definition: Violently rotating column of air extending from thunderstorm to ground, most intense of all atmospheric phenomena.

Enhanced Fujita Scale:

  • EF0: 105-137 km/h (light damage)
  • EF1: 138-178 km/h (moderate)
  • EF2: 179-218 km/h (considerable)
  • EF3: 219-266 km/h (severe)
  • EF4: 267-322 km/h (devastating)
  • EF5: >322 km/h (incredible)

Tornado Alley: USA Great Plains - 1,000+ tornadoes/year

🌿 Environmental Issues - Complete Encyclopedia

Understanding humanity's impact on Earth's ecosystems

1. 🌲 Deforestation

Simple Explanation

Imagine forests are Earth's lungs - they breathe in bad air (CO₂) and breathe out good air (oxygen). Deforestation is like cutting away those lungs! When we cut too many trees for wood, farms, or cities, animals lose their homes, and Earth has trouble breathing. It's like removing the AC from a hot room!

📚 Scientific Definition

Deforestation is the permanent removal of forest cover and conversion to non-forest land use, including agriculture, urbanization, and infrastructure development. It represents one of the most significant anthropogenic alterations of Earth's terrestrial ecosystems, with profound impacts on biodiversity, climate regulation, and biogeochemical cycles.

📊 Global Statistics

  • Annual Loss: 10 million hectares (area of Iceland) deforested yearly (2015-2020)
  • Total Forest Loss: 420 million hectares since 1990 (10% of remaining forests)
  • Rate: Equivalent to 27 football fields of forest lost every minute
  • Primary Forest Loss: 3.8 million hectares/year of irreplaceable old-growth forests

🌍 Major Causes

  • Agricultural Expansion (80%): Cattle ranching, soy farming, palm oil plantations
  • Logging Operations (14%): Legal and illegal timber harvesting
  • Infrastructure (5%): Roads, dams, mining, urban sprawl
  • Wildfires (1%): Often human-induced for land clearing

🌏 Hotspots

  • Amazon Rainforest: Brazil lost 1.5 million hectares in 2021 alone
  • Congo Basin: Africa's "second lung" losing 1.5% forest cover annually
  • Southeast Asia: Indonesia/Malaysia lose rainforest to palm oil (98% loss since 1900)
  • Madagascar: 90% original forest destroyed since human arrival

⚠️ Consequences

  • Climate Impact: 15% of global greenhouse gas emissions from deforestation
  • Biodiversity Loss: 80% of terrestrial species live in forests - facing extinction
  • Soil Erosion: Forest soils erode 20x faster after tree removal
  • Water Cycle Disruption: Reduced rainfall, increased flooding
  • Indigenous Communities: 300 million people displaced from forest homes
🔬 Research Insight: A single large tree can provide oxygen for 2 people per day, absorb 22kg of CO₂ annually, and provide habitat for 1,000+ species. The Amazon alone produces 20% of Earth's oxygen and contains 10% of all species on the planet!

🏜️ Desertification

Definition: Land degradation in arid/semi-arid areas due to climate change and human activities, affecting 3.2 billion people.

Causes:

  • Overgrazing (35% of cases)
  • Deforestation (30%)
  • Agricultural practices (27%)
  • Climate change (intensifier)

Scale: 12 million hectares degraded annually (23 hectares/minute)

Affected Regions: Sub-Saharan Africa (Sahel), Central Asia, Middle East, Australian outback

Impact: $490 billion annual economic losses

💨 Air Pollution

Definition: Introduction of harmful substances into atmosphere, killing 7 million people annually (WHO).

Major Pollutants:

  • PM2.5/PM10 (particulate matter)
  • CO₂, CO (carbon compounds)
  • NOₓ (nitrogen oxides)
  • SO₂ (sulfur dioxide)
  • O₃ (ground-level ozone)
  • VOCs (volatile organic compounds)

Sources: Vehicles (40%), industry (30%), power generation (20%), agriculture (10%)

Worst Cities: Delhi, Beijing, Cairo, Dhaka, Mumbai

💧 Water Pollution

Definition: Contamination of water bodies making them toxic to humans and ecosystems, affecting 2 billion people globally.

Types:

  • Chemical: Pesticides, industrial waste
  • Biological: Bacteria, viruses, parasites
  • Physical: Plastic, sediment
  • Nutrient: Nitrogen/phosphorus (eutrophication)

Sources: Industrial discharge (40%), agricultural runoff (33%), sewage (27%)

Impact: 1.8 million child deaths/year from waterborne diseases

🏭 Soil Pollution

Definition: Accumulation of toxic substances in soil exceeding natural levels, degrading 33% of Earth's soils.

Contaminants:

  • Heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic)
  • Pesticides/herbicides
  • Industrial chemicals
  • Petroleum hydrocarbons
  • Radioactive materials

Health Impact: 95% of food grown in contaminated soil

Recovery: Can take 500-1,000 years naturally

🔊 Noise Pollution

Definition: Harmful levels of ambient sound (>85 dB prolonged) disrupting human/animal life, affecting 466 million people with hearing loss.

Sources:

  • Traffic (urban: 70-90 dB)
  • Aircraft (takeoff: 120 dB)
  • Construction (110-120 dB)
  • Industry (80-100 dB)

Health Effects: Hearing loss, cardiovascular disease, sleep disturbance, stress

Wildlife Impact: Disrupts mating, migration, hunting

🌐 Ozone Depletion

Definition: Thinning of stratospheric ozone layer (15-35 km altitude) protecting Earth from UV radiation.

Cause: Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), halons, used in refrigeration/aerosols until Montreal Protocol (1987)

Antarctic Ozone Hole: Discovered 1985, reached 29.9 million km² (2000) - 3× size of USA

Recovery: Expected by 2070 if protocol compliance continues

Impact Prevented: 2 million skin cancer cases/year avoided

🌡️ Global Warming

Definition: Long-term rise in Earth's average temperature due to increased greenhouse gases, up 1.1°C since pre-industrial era.

Key GHGs:

  • CO₂ (76%): 419 ppm (2021), highest in 3M years
  • CH₄ (16%): Methane from agriculture/fossil fuels
  • N₂O (6%): Nitrous oxide from fertilizers
  • F-gases (2%): Industrial fluorinated gases

Rate: Warming 0.2°C per decade since 1975

Target: Limit to 1.5°C above pre-industrial (Paris Agreement)

🌊 Rising Sea Levels

Definition: Increase in ocean surface elevation due to thermal expansion and ice melt, risen 21-24 cm since 1880.

Causes:

  • Thermal expansion (50%): Water expands when warmed
  • Glacier melt (25%): Mountain glaciers worldwide
  • Ice sheet loss (25%): Greenland/Antarctica

Current Rate: 3.3 mm/year (accelerating)

Projections: 0.3-1.0 meter rise by 2100

Risk: 230 million people in coastal areas <1m elevation

🛍️ Plastic Pollution

Definition: Accumulation of synthetic polymer objects harming wildlife/ecosystems/humans, 8.3 billion tonnes produced since 1950s.

Scale:

  • Annual production: 380 million tonnes
  • Ocean plastic: 8 million tonnes/year
  • Great Pacific Garbage Patch: 1.6M km²
  • Microplastics: In 83% tap water globally

Lifespan: Plastic bottles: 450 years, bags: 10-1,000 years

Recycled: Only 9% of all plastic ever made

☔ Acid Rain

Definition: Precipitation with pH <5.6 due to atmospheric sulfuric/nitric acids, damaging ecosystems and infrastructure.

Formation: SO₂ + NOₓ (from fossil fuel combustion) + H₂O → H₂SO₄/HNO₃

Effects:

  • Aquatic ecosystems: pH <4.5 kills fish
  • Forest damage: Leaches soil nutrients
  • Infrastructure: Erodes marble/limestone

Improvement: 70% reduction in sulfate deposition (USA) since Clean Air Act (1970)

🦋 Loss of Biodiversity

Definition: Decline in variety of life at genetic/species/ecosystem levels, currently in "Sixth Mass Extinction."

Extinction Rate: 1,000x natural background rate - 200 species/day (estimate)

Red List Status:

  • 41% amphibians threatened
  • 26% mammals threatened
  • 14% birds threatened
  • 37% sharks/rays threatened

Drivers: Habitat loss (70%), overexploitation (20%), invasive species, pollution, climate change

🪸 Coral Bleaching

Definition: Coral expulsion of symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae) due to stress, leaving white skeleton visible.

Primary Cause: Sea temperature rise >1-2°C above normal for 4+ weeks

Global Impact:

  • 2016-2017: Third global bleaching event
  • Great Barrier Reef: 50% coral died (2016-2017)
  • Caribbean: 80% coral cover lost since 1970s

Consequence: 25% of marine species depend on reefs; 500M people rely on them

🏔️ Glacial Retreat

Definition: Worldwide shrinking of glaciers due to climate warming, losing 267 billion tonnes ice/year.

Statistics:

  • Glacier National Park: 150→25 glaciers (since 1850)
  • Alps: Lost 60% ice volume (1850-2020)
  • Himalayas: 1/3 ice will melt by 2100
  • Kilimanjaro: Lost 85% ice cap (1912-2020)

Impact: 2 billion people depend on glacier meltwater

Sea Level Contribution: ~21% of current rise

🏔️ India's Natural Disaster Profile

Earthquake Zones

  • Zone V (Very High Risk): Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, parts of Bihar, Northeast states
  • Zone IV (High Risk): Delhi, NCR, Jammu, remaining Bihar, northern West Bengal, parts of Gujarat, Rajasthan
  • Zone III (Moderate Risk): Kerala, Goa, most of Rajasthan, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal, Punjab, Haryana
  • Zone II (Low Risk): Karnataka, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Odisha, Tamil Nadu

Flood-Prone States (Annual Impact)

  • Most Affected: Bihar, Assam, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal, Odisha
  • Area Prone: 40 million hectares (12% of land)
  • Average Annual Damage: ₹7,000 crore ($1 billion)
  • Major Floods: Brahmaputra (Assam), Ganga-Yamuna (UP/Bihar), Mahanadi (Odisha)

Cyclone-Prone Coastal States

  • East Coast (Bay of Bengal): Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, West Bengal - 80% of all cyclones
  • West Coast (Arabian Sea): Gujarat, Maharashtra, Kerala, Karnataka - 20% of cyclones
  • Frequency: 5-6 cyclones annually, 2-3 severe
  • Recent Major: Cyclone Fani (2019), Amphan (2020), Yaas (2021), Tauktae (2021)

Drought-Prone Regions

  • Severely Affected: Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana
  • Area: 68% of India prone to drought
  • Impact: 50 million people affected annually

Landslide-Prone Areas

  • Himalayan States: Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh
  • Western Ghats: Kerala, Karnataka, Maharashtra
  • Eastern Ghats: Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh
  • Northeast: All seven sister states

🌿 India's Environmental Challenges

Air Pollution (Most Critical)

  • Top 10 Most Polluted Cities (2021 WHO): Delhi, Ghaziabad, Faridabad, Noida, Gurugram, Greater Noida, Muzaffarnagar, Baghpat, Jind, Hisar
  • PM2.5 Levels: Delhi averages 110 µg/m³ (11x WHO safe limit of 10 µg/m³)
  • Health Impact: 1.67 million deaths annually attributed to air pollution
  • Economic Cost: $95 billion/year (3% of GDP)
  • Sources: Vehicle emissions (40%), crop burning (25%), industrial (20%), construction (15%)

Deforestation

  • Forest Cover: 713,789 km² (21.71% of geographical area) - target is 33%
  • Annual Loss: ~1,500 km²/year to agriculture, urbanization, mining
  • Most Affected States: Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Maharashtra, Assam
  • Tribal Displacement: 8 million tribal people depend on forests

Water Pollution

  • River Health: 351 polluted river stretches across India
  • Ganga Pollution: 2.9 billion liters of sewage daily, only 40% treated
  • Groundwater: 60% aquifers contaminated (arsenic, fluoride, nitrates, heavy metals)
  • Disease Burden: 37.7 million affected by waterborne diseases annually

Plastic Waste

  • Generation: 3.5 million tonnes/year (340,000 tonnes single-use plastic)
  • Collection Rate: Only 60% collected
  • Top Generators: Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai
  • Ocean Impact: India among top 5 marine plastic polluters

Soil Degradation

  • Affected Area: 120.4 million hectares (37% of geographical area)
  • Types: Water erosion (82.6 Mha), wind erosion (10.3 Mha), salinization (6.7 Mha)
  • Agricultural Impact: Productivity loss worth $3 billion/year

Climate Change Impacts

  • Temperature Rise: 0.7°C since 1900, projected 2-4.5°C by 2100
  • Glacier Retreat: Himalayan glaciers losing 7% volume per decade
  • Sea Level Rise: 1.3 mm/year, threatening Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai
  • Monsoon Variability: Increased erratic rainfall patterns
  • Economic Risk: 2.8% GDP loss/year by 2050 (ADB estimate)

Biodiversity Loss

  • Species Count: 45,000 plant species, 91,000 animal species (8% global biodiversity)
  • Threatened Species: 1,226 species on IUCN Red List (9.4% of India's fauna/flora)
  • Critically Endangered: Great Indian Bustard (<200 left), Kashmir Stag (~200), Pygmy Hog (<250)
  • Habitat Loss: 90% wetlands lost in Gangetic plains since 1950s

Coral Reef Degradation

  • Locations: Andaman & Nicobar (11,000 km²), Lakshadweep (200 km²), Gulf of Mannar, Gulf of Kutch
  • Bleaching Events: 2016 bleached 60% of reefs, 2019 affected 40%
  • Threats: Rising sea temperatures, sedimentation, destructive fishing, pollution

🛡️ India's Disaster Management Framework

National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)

  • Established: 2005 (Disaster Management Act)
  • Leadership: Prime Minister as Chairman
  • Functions: Policy formulation, coordination, early warning systems

State Disaster Response Forces (SDRF)

  • Strength: 12 battalions deployed across states
  • Training: Specialized in flood rescue, earthquake response, cyclone management

Early Warning Systems

  • Cyclone: Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) - 24-48 hour advance warning
  • Tsunami: Indian Tsunami Early Warning Centre (ITEWC) - 10-30 minute warning
  • Flood: Central Water Commission (CWC) monitors 17,000 stations
  • Earthquake: National Centre for Seismology operates 115 seismic stations

Environmental Initiatives

  • Namami Gange: ₹20,000 crore to clean Ganga by 2024
  • National Clean Air Programme: 20-30% pollution reduction by 2024
  • Green India Mission: 5 million hectares afforestation target
  • Plastic Ban: Single-use plastic banned from July 2022
  • EV Push: 30% electric vehicle penetration by 2030
  • Solar Mission: 450 GW renewable energy by 2030
  • Climate Pledge: Net-zero emissions by 2070 (COP26)
