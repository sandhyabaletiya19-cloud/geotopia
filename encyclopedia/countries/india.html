India's southernmost point Indira Point in the Andaman & Nicobar Islands was submerged during the 2004 Tsunami. The standard meridian 82°30'E passes through Mirzapur, Uttar Pradesh, determining IST (Indian Standard Time).

Branch 02

Physical Divisions of India

The Himalayan Region

  • Length: ~2,400 km arc
  • Width: 150-400 km
  • Three parallel ranges — Greater, Lesser, Outer
  • Young fold mountains — Still rising
  • Source of major perennial rivers
  • Natural barrier against cold winds

Northern Plains

  • Area: ~7 lakh km²
  • Formed by Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra systems
  • Extremely fertile alluvial soil
  • Most densely populated region
  • Divided into: Bhabar, Terai, Bhangar, Khadar
  • India's agricultural heartland

Peninsular Plateau

  • Oldest landmass — Part of Gondwanaland
  • Triangular tableland
  • Central Highlands + Deccan Plateau
  • Rich in minerals — Iron, Coal, Manganese
  • Average elevation: 600-900m
  • Black soil region — Cotton cultivation

Indian Desert (Thar)

  • Located in Rajasthan
  • Area: ~2 lakh km²
  • Sandy plains with shifting dunes
  • Rainfall: < 150 mm annually
  • Luni — Only significant river
  • Indira Gandhi Canal — Lifeline

Coastal Plains

  • Western Coast: Konkan, Malabar
  • Eastern Coast: Coromandel, Northern Circars
  • Eastern coast wider than Western
  • Major deltas on Eastern coast
  • Lagoons & backwaters — Kerala
  • Important for ports & fishing

Islands

  • Andaman & Nicobar: Bay of Bengal
  • Lakshadweep: Arabian Sea
  • A&N — Volcanic origin, 572 islands
  • Lakshadweep — Coral origin, 36 islands
  • Only active volcano — Barren Island
  • Strategic importance — Maritime security
Did You Know?

The Peninsular Plateau is one of the oldest landmasses on Earth, dating back over 3.8 billion years. It was once part of the supercontinent Gondwanaland!

Branch 03

Major Mountain Systems

The Himalayas

  • Himadri: Greater Himalayas — Highest, perpetual snow
  • Himachal: Lesser Himalayas — 3,700-4,500m
  • Shiwaliks: Outer Himalayas — 900-1,100m
  • Important valleys: Kashmir, Kullu, Kangra
  • Famous passes: Khyber, Bolan, Shipki La, Nathu La

Western Ghats (Sahyadris)

  • Continuous chain — 1,600 km
  • From Tapi Valley to Kanyakumari
  • Average height: 1,000-1,500m
  • Biodiversity hotspot — UNESCO site
  • Highest peak: Anaimudi (2,695m)
  • Important gaps: Thal, Bhor, Pal Ghat

Eastern Ghats

  • Discontinuous hills — Broken by rivers
  • From Mahanadi to Vaigai
  • Average height: 600m
  • Highest peak: Mahendragiri (1,501m)
  • Merge with Western Ghats at Nilgiri Hills

Important Peaks

  • K2 (8,611m): India's highest (in PoK)
  • Kanchenjunga (8,586m): Highest in India proper
  • Nanda Devi (7,816m): Uttarakhand
  • Kamet (7,756m): Uttarakhand
  • Anaimudi (2,695m): South India's highest
  • Dodda Betta (2,637m): Nilgiris highest
UPSC Important Points
  • Western Ghats are higher and more continuous than Eastern Ghats
  • Palghat Gap — Important passage connecting Kerala & Tamil Nadu
  • Aravalli Range — Oldest fold mountains in India
  • Shiwaliks are also called Outer Himalayas or Sub-Himalayas
Branch 04

Rivers and Drainage System

Himalayan Rivers

  • Perennial — Fed by snow & rain
  • Indus System: Indus, Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Sutlej
  • Ganga System: Ganga, Yamuna, Gomti, Ghaghara, Gandak, Kosi
  • Brahmaputra System: Brahmaputra, Teesta, Subansiri
  • Form large fertile plains
  • Meandering course with ox-bow lakes

Peninsular Rivers

  • Seasonal — Rain-fed only
  • East flowing: Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri
  • West flowing: Narmada, Tapi
  • Straight, shallow valleys
  • Major deltas on East coast
  • Narmada & Tapi flow through rift valleys

Major River Basins

  • Ganga Basin: 26% of India — Largest
  • Godavari Basin: Largest peninsular basin
  • Indus Basin: Northwest India
  • Brahmaputra Basin: Northeast region
  • Krishna Basin: 2nd largest peninsular
  • 12 major river basins in India

Important Deltas

  • Sundarbans: Ganga-Brahmaputra — World's largest
  • Godavari Delta: Andhra Pradesh
  • Krishna Delta: Andhra Pradesh
  • Mahanadi Delta: Odisha
  • Kaveri Delta: Tamil Nadu — Granary of South

Did You Know?

The Brahmaputra is known by different names in different regions: Tsangpo in Tibet, Dihang in Arunachal Pradesh, and Jamuna in Bangladesh. It's one of the few rivers that flow from east to west!

Branch 05

Climate System

Monsoon System

  • Southwest Monsoon: June-September (85% rainfall)
  • Northeast Monsoon: Oct-Dec (Tamil Nadu)
  • Driven by pressure difference — Land & Sea
  • Arabian Sea branch — Western coast
  • Bay of Bengal branch — Eastern & NE India
  • Inter-Tropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ) key factor

Seasons of India

  • Winter: Dec-Feb — Cool, dry NE winds
  • Summer: Mar-May — Hot, dry, Loo winds
  • Monsoon: Jun-Sep — Heavy rainfall
  • Post-Monsoon: Oct-Nov — Retreating monsoon
Summer: Up to 50°C Winter: -40°C (Ladakh)

Climate Zones

  • Tropical Wet: Western Ghats, NE India
  • Tropical Wet & Dry: Most of Peninsula
  • Semi-Arid: Gujarat, Rajasthan, parts of Karnataka
  • Arid/Desert: Western Rajasthan
  • Subtropical Humid: Northern Plains
  • Mountain Climate: Himalayas

Major Climate Influences

  • Himalayas: Block cold Central Asian winds
  • Indian Ocean: Moderates coastal temperatures
  • Jet Streams: Upper air circulation patterns
  • El Niño/La Niña: Affects monsoon intensity
  • Western Disturbances: Winter rainfall in North
Did You Know?

Mawsynram in Meghalaya receives the highest average annual rainfall in the world (~11,871 mm). Its neighbor Cherrapunji holds the record for the highest rainfall in a single year!

Branch 06

Natural Vegetation

Tropical Evergreen Forests

  • Rainfall: > 200 cm annually
  • Dense, multi-layered canopy
  • Locations: Western Ghats, NE India, Andaman
  • Trees: Ebony, Mahogany, Rosewood, Rubber
  • No definite time for leaf shedding

Deciduous Forests

  • Rainfall: 100-200 cm
  • Shed leaves in dry season
  • Moist Deciduous: Teak, Sal, Bamboo, Sandalwood
  • Dry Deciduous: Neem, Peepal, Acacia
  • Most widespread forest type in India

Thorn Forests

  • Rainfall: < 75 cm
  • Scrubland vegetation
  • Locations: Rajasthan, Gujarat, MP, UP
  • Plants: Babul, Khair, Wild Palms, Cactus
  • Adapted to drought conditions

Mangrove Forests

  • Coastal tidal zones
  • Largest: Sundarbans (UNESCO Site)
  • Salt-tolerant species
  • Dense aerial root systems
  • Important for coastal protection
  • Habitat for Royal Bengal Tiger

Alpine Vegetation

  • Found: Above 3,600m in Himalayas
  • Silver Fir, Juniper, Birch, Rhododendrons
  • At higher altitudes — Alpine grasslands
  • Mosses and lichens near snow line
Forest Cover: 24.62% (2021) Target: 33% MP Highest Forest Cover
Branch 07

Soils of India

Alluvial Soil

  • Coverage: ~43% of soil area — Largest
  • Deposited by rivers — Highly fertile
  • Bhangar: Old alluvium, higher elevation
  • Khadar: New alluvium, flood plains
  • Crops: Rice, Wheat, Sugarcane, Pulses
  • Found in Northern Plains, Coastal areas

Black Soil (Regur)

  • Derived from basalt rocks
  • Rich in lime, iron, magnesium
  • High moisture retention capacity
  • Best for: Cotton — "Black Cotton Soil"
  • Found: Maharashtra, Gujarat, MP, Karnataka

Red Soil

  • Red color due to iron oxide
  • Formed from crystalline rocks
  • Low nitrogen, phosphorus content
  • Crops: Millets, Groundnut, Tobacco
  • Found: Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Odisha, MP

Laterite Soil

  • Result of intense leaching
  • Rich in iron & aluminum, poor in nitrogen
  • Acidic in nature
  • Crops: Tea, Coffee, Cashew, Rubber
  • Found: Western Ghats, Odisha, Assam hills

Desert Soil

  • Sandy, porous, saline
  • Low organic content
  • Lacks moisture & humus
  • Found: Western Rajasthan, parts of Gujarat
  • Can be productive with irrigation

Mountain Soil

  • Found in Himalayan region
  • Rich in humus, but acidic
  • Suitable for tea, fruits
  • Prone to erosion
  • Found: J&K, HP, Uttarakhand, NE states
Branch 08

Agriculture Geography

Major Crops

  • Rice: WB, UP, Punjab — 2nd largest producer globally
  • Wheat: UP, Punjab, Haryana — Main rabi crop
  • Cotton: Gujarat, Maharashtra, Telangana
  • Sugarcane: UP, Maharashtra, Karnataka
  • Tea: Assam, West Bengal, Tamil Nadu
  • Coffee: Karnataka (70%), Kerala, Tamil Nadu

Green Revolution Regions

  • Punjab-Haryana: Wheat revolution hub
  • Tamil Nadu: Early rice adoption
  • Started in 1966-67
  • Father: M.S. Swaminathan
  • HYV seeds, fertilizers, irrigation focus
  • Made India self-sufficient in food grains

Irrigation Systems

  • Canal Irrigation: UP, Punjab, Haryana
  • Well/Tube Well: Most common — UP, Rajasthan, Gujarat
  • Tank Irrigation: South India — TN, AP, Karnataka
  • Net irrigated area: ~49% of cropped area
  • Major projects: Bhakra Nangal, Indira Gandhi Canal

Agro-Climatic Zones

  • India divided into 15 zones by Planning Commission
  • Western Himalayan: Apple, walnut
  • Lower Gangetic: Rice, jute
  • Western Plateau: Cotton, sorghum
  • Coastal Plains: Rice, coconut
42% Workforce in Agriculture 18% GDP Contribution 2nd Largest Producer
Branch 09

Mineral and Resource Geography

Coal

  • Largest reserves: Jharkhand (Jharia)
  • Other states: Odisha, Chhattisgarh, WB, MP
  • Types: Anthracite (rare), Bituminous (common)
  • Gondwana coalfields — 98% of production
  • 5th largest reserves globally

Iron Ore

  • Largest reserves: Odisha, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh
  • Singhbhum — Major belt
  • Types: Hematite (best), Magnetite
  • 4th largest reserves globally
  • Karnataka — Bellary-Hospet region

Bauxite

  • Leading producer: Odisha
  • Other: Gujarat, Jharkhand, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh
  • Used for aluminum production
  • Panchpatmali — Largest deposit

Petroleum & Natural Gas

  • Mumbai High: Largest offshore field
  • Assam: Digboi (oldest), Naharkatiya
  • Gujarat: Ankleshwar, Cambay
  • Krishna-Godavari Basin: Major gas reserves
  • India imports ~85% of crude oil needs

Renewable Energy Regions

  • Solar: Rajasthan, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu
  • Wind: Tamil Nadu (#1), Gujarat, Karnataka
  • Hydro: Himalayan states, NE India
  • Bhadla Solar Park — World's largest
  • Target: 500 GW renewable by 2030
UPSC Important Points
  • Jharkhand is called "Ruhr of India" due to mineral wealth
  • Neyveli in Tamil Nadu has India's major lignite deposits
  • Mica — Jharkhand leads (75% production)
  • Manganese — Odisha, Maharashtra, MP are top producers
Branch 10

Population Geography

Population Distribution

  • Total: ~1.42 billion (2024) — Most populous
  • Highest: UP (200+ million)
  • Lowest: Lakshadweep
  • Density: 382 per km²
  • Indo-Gangetic Plain — Densest region
  • NE & Himalayan regions — Sparse

Urbanization Patterns

  • Urban population: ~35%
  • Most urbanized state: Goa (~63%)
  • Least urbanized: Himachal Pradesh
  • Mega cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Bangalore
  • Rural-urban migration increasing

Major Metropolitan Regions

  • Mumbai: Financial capital (~20M+)
  • Delhi NCR: Political hub (~32M+)
  • Kolkata: Eastern gateway (~14M+)
  • Chennai: Auto hub (~11M+)
  • Bangalore: IT capital (~13M+)
  • Hyderabad: Pharma & IT hub (~10M+)

  • East Coast: Chennai, Visakhapatnam, Paradip, Haldia, Kolkata
  • Largest: JNPT (Nhava Sheva)

Branch 12

Coastal and Marine Geography

Major Gulfs

  • Gulf of Khambhat: Gujarat — Tidal energy potential
  • Gulf of Kutch: Gujarat — Marine National Park
  • Gulf of Mannar: Tamil Nadu — Biosphere Reserve

Major Straits

  • Palk Strait: India & Sri Lanka
  • Ten Degree Channel: Andaman & Nicobar
  • Eight Degree Channel: Lakshadweep & Maldives
  • Nine Degree Channel: Within Lakshadweep

Coastal Ecosystems

  • Sundarbans: Largest mangrove — UNESCO site
  • Gulf of Mannar: Coral reefs, dugongs
  • Chilika Lake: Largest coastal lagoon (Odisha)
  • Kerala Backwaters: Unique ecosystem
  • 9 coastal states + 4 UTs
Did You Know?

India has an Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) of over 2 million km² — almost 2/3rd of its land area! The Andaman & Nicobar Islands give India strategic presence in the Bay of Bengal.

Branch 13

Natural Hazards

Earthquake Zones

  • Zone V (Very High): NE India, J&K, HP, Uttarakhand, Kutch
  • Zone IV (High): Delhi, UP, Bihar, parts of Maharashtra
  • Zone III (Moderate): Kerala, Goa, coastal areas
  • Zone II (Low): Interior Peninsula
  • 59% of land susceptible to earthquakes

Flood-Prone Regions

  • Bihar: Kosi River — "Sorrow of Bihar"
  • Assam: Brahmaputra floods annual
  • West Bengal: Gangetic delta region
  • UP: Ganga-Yamuna plains
  • ~40 million hectares flood-prone

Cyclone-Prone Coasts

  • East Coast: More vulnerable — Odisha, AP, TN, WB
  • West Coast: Gujarat, Maharashtra
  • October-December — Post-monsoon cyclones
  • April-June — Pre-monsoon cyclones
  • Bay of Bengal generates 5x more cyclones than Arabian Sea

Landslide Regions

  • Himalayan states: HP, Uttarakhand, J&K, Sikkim
  • Northeast: Assam, Meghalaya, Nagaland
  • Western Ghats: Kerala, Karnataka, Maharashtra
  • Triggered by heavy rainfall, deforestation
