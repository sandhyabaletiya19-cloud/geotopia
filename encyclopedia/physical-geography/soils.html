What is Soil?

Understanding the foundation of life on Earth

🧒 For Young Explorers

Soil is like a magical blanket that covers the ground! It's made of tiny pieces of rocks, dead leaves, water, air, and millions of tiny creatures you can't see. Plants grow their roots in soil to drink water and eat food, just like you use a straw to drink juice!

🎂 Think of it like a Layer Cake!

Soil has different layers, just like a birthday cake! The top layer (frosting) is dark and full of yummy stuff for plants. The middle layers are like the cake - they have different colors and hold water. The bottom layer is like the cake plate - it's the hard rock underneath!

🐛

Did You Know?

One teaspoon of healthy soil contains more living organisms than there are people on Earth! That's more than 8 billion tiny creatures including bacteria, fungi, and microscopic animals - all working together to keep plants healthy!

🎓 Scientific Definition

Soil (from Latin solum, meaning "floor" or "ground") is defined as the unconsolidated mineral or organic material on the immediate surface of the Earth that serves as a natural medium for the growth of land plants. It is a complex three-dimensional natural body resulting from the integrated effects of climate, living matter, and topography acting on parent material over time.

Pedologically, soil is distinguished from regolith (the layer of loose, heterogeneous material covering solid rock) by its biological activity and the presence of distinct horizons formed through pedogenic processes including weathering, leaching, translocation, and organic matter accumulation.

Pedosphere

The outermost layer of Earth composed of soil and subject to soil formation processes

Edaphology

The study of soil in relation to plant growth and agriculture

Pedology

The scientific study of soil formation, classification, and mapping

Soil Profile

A vertical section of soil showing all its horizons from surface to parent material

Horizons

Distinct layers in soil (O, A, E, B, C, R) with different characteristics

Pedogenesis

The process of soil formation and development over time

How Soil Forms

A journey spanning thousands to millions of years

Stage 1: 0-100 Years

🪨 Physical Weathering

Parent rock is broken down by temperature changes, water freezing in cracks, and root growth. Large rocks become smaller fragments through mechanical processes.

Stage 2: 100-500 Years

🧪 Chemical Weathering

Minerals in rocks react with water and air, dissolving and transforming. Acid rain and organic acids accelerate this process, creating clay particles.

Stage 3: 500-2,000 Years

🌿 Pioneer Plants Arrive

Lichens, mosses, and small plants colonize the weathered rock. Their roots break rock further, and dead material begins forming organic matter.

Stage 4: 2,000-10,000 Years

🐜 Biological Activity Increases

Earthworms, insects, and microorganisms move in. They mix organic matter with minerals, creating the first true soil horizons.

Stage 5: 10,000-100,000 Years

📊 Horizon Development

Distinct soil layers (horizons) form. Water moves minerals and organic compounds up and down the profile, creating the characteristic A, B, and C horizons.

Stage 6: 100,000+ Years

✨ Mature Soil

The soil reaches dynamic equilibrium with its environment. It can maintain fertility through natural cycles and supports complex ecosystems.

The 12 Soil Orders

USDA Soil Taxonomy Classification System

Alfisols

Order #1 | 9.6% of Earth's Land

The Fertile Forest Soils - Alfisols form under deciduous forests and have clay-enriched subsoils. They're among the most productive agricultural soils worldwide.

Clay-rich B horizon Moderate-high fertility Forest origin
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇺🇸 USA
  • 🇫🇷 France
  • 🇮🇳 India
  • 🇨🇳 China
  • 🇦🇺 Australia

Andisols

Order #2 | 0.7% of Earth's Land

The Volcanic Soils - Formed from volcanic ash, Andisols are lightweight, porous, and extremely fertile. They support intensive agriculture in volcanic regions.

Volcanic origin High water retention Very fertile
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇯🇵 Japan
  • 🇮🇩 Indonesia
  • 🇵🇭 Philippines
  • 🇪🇨 Ecuador
  • 🇳🇿 New Zealand

Aridisols

Order #3 | 12.0% of Earth's Land

The Desert Soils - Found in arid regions with less than 250mm annual rainfall. Low organic matter, often with salt and calcium carbonate accumulations.

Low organic matter Salt accumulation Dry climate
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia
  • 🇦🇺 Australia
  • 🇲🇦 Morocco
  • 🇺🇸 USA (SW)
  • 🇮🇳 India (Thar)

Entisols

Order #4 | 16.2% of Earth's Land

The Young Soils - Recently formed soils with no horizon development. Found on steep slopes, floodplains, and areas of recent deposition or erosion.

No horizons Recently formed Variable fertility
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇪🇬 Egypt (Nile)
  • 🇧🇩 Bangladesh
  • 🇳🇵 Nepal
  • 🇺🇸 USA (Rivers)
  • 🇨🇳 China

Gelisols

Order #5 | 8.6% of Earth's Land

The Permafrost Soils - Contains permafrost within 2 meters of surface. Found in arctic and high alpine regions. Stores vast amounts of carbon.

Permafrost Carbon storage Cryoturbation
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇷🇺 Russia
  • 🇨🇦 Canada
  • 🇺🇸 Alaska
  • 🇬🇱 Greenland
  • 🇳🇴 Norway

Histosols

Order #6 | 1.2% of Earth's Land

The Organic Soils - Composed mainly of organic matter (peat, muck). Form in wetlands where decomposition is slow. Important carbon reservoirs.

Organic-rich Wetland origin Carbon sink
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇷🇺 Russia
  • 🇨🇦 Canada
  • 🇮🇩 Indonesia
  • 🇫🇮 Finland
  • 🇮🇪 Ireland

Inceptisols

Order #7 | 9.8% of Earth's Land

The Developing Soils - Young soils with beginning horizon development. Found in humid regions on steep slopes. Moderate fertility.

Weak horizons Humid climate Moderate fertility
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇮🇳 India
  • 🇧🇷 Brazil
  • 🇨🇳 China
  • 🇺🇸 USA
  • 🇮🇩 Indonesia

Mollisols

Order #8 | 6.9% of Earth's Land

The Prairie Soils - Dark, fertile grassland soils with thick A horizon. World's most productive agricultural soils. High organic matter content.

Dark surface High fertility Grassland origin
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇺🇦 Ukraine
  • 🇺🇸 USA (Midwest)
  • 🇦🇷 Argentina
  • 🇷🇺 Russia
  • 🇨🇳 China

Oxisols

Order #9 | 7.5% of Earth's Land

The Tropical Red Soils - Highly weathered soils in humid tropics. Rich in iron and aluminum oxides. Low natural fertility due to intense leaching.

Highly weathered Low fertility Iron-rich
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇧🇷 Brazil
  • 🇨🇩 DR Congo
  • 🇮🇩 Indonesia
  • 🇨🇴 Colombia
  • 🇻🇳 Vietnam

Spodosols

Order #10 | 2.6% of Earth's Land

The Coniferous Forest Soils - Acidic soils with ash-gray E horizon and dark B horizon. Form under coniferous forests in cool, humid climates.

Acidic Sandy texture Leached
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇷🇺 Russia
  • 🇨🇦 Canada
  • 🇸🇪 Sweden
  • 🇫🇮 Finland
  • 🇺🇸 USA (NE)

Ultisols

Order #11 | 8.5% of Earth's Land

The Humid Subtropical Soils - Highly leached soils with clay accumulation in B horizon. Acidic with low base saturation. Require fertilization for agriculture.

Leached Acidic Clay-rich
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇺🇸 USA (SE)
  • 🇨🇳 China
  • 🇧🇷 Brazil
  • 🇮🇳 India
  • 🇻🇳 Vietnam

Vertisols

Order #12 | 2.4% of Earth's Land

The Swelling Clay Soils - High clay content that swells when wet and shrinks when dry, creating deep cracks. Challenging for construction but fertile for crops.

High clay Shrink-swell Self-mixing
📍 Major Distribution:
  • 🇮🇳 India
  • 🇦🇺 Australia
  • 🇸🇩 Sudan
  • 🇺🇸 Texas
  • 🇪🇹 Ethiopia

🇮🇳 Soils of India

India has 8 major soil types covering diverse climatic zones

🟫 Alluvial Soil

43% of India
  • Found in Indo-Gangetic plains
  • Most fertile, supports rice and wheat
  • Two types: Khadar (new) & Bhangar (old)
  • States: UP, Bihar, Punjab, Haryana, WB

⬛ Black Soil (Regur)

15% of India
  • Formed from Deccan basalt
  • Self-ploughing, high moisture retention
  • Ideal for cotton cultivation
  • States: Maharashtra, Gujarat, MP, Karnataka

🔴 Red Soil

18.5% of India
  • Rich in iron, poor in nitrogen
  • Porous with low water retention
  • Good for groundnut, tobacco, millets
  • States: TN, Karnataka, Odisha, Jharkhand

🟡 Laterite Soil

3.7% of India
  • High rainfall areas, acidic nature
  • Rich in iron and aluminum
  • Suitable for tea, coffee, cashew
  • States: Kerala, Assam, Karnataka, WB Hills

🏜️ Desert Soil

6.4% of India
  • Sandy texture, low organic matter
  • High salt content, rapid drainage
  • Bajra, guar, dates with irrigation
  • States: Rajasthan, Gujarat, Haryana

🏔️ Mountain Soil

8.6% of India
  • Varies with altitude and climate
  • Rich in humus, acidic in nature
  • Tea, coffee, spices, fruits
  • States: Himalayan states, NE India

🧂 Saline & Alkaline Soil

2.5% of India
  • High salt concentration (Reh/Usar)
  • Poor drainage areas, waterlogged
  • Requires gypsum for reclamation
  • States: Punjab, Haryana, Gujarat, UP

🌿 Peaty & Marshy Soil

2.3% of India
  • Highly organic, formed in wetlands
  • Acidic, black, heavy texture
  • Good for rice cultivation
  • States: Kerala, WB, Odisha, TN coast
