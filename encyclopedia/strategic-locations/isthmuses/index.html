About Isthmuses

An isthmus is a narrow strip of land that connects two larger landmasses and separates two bodies of water. Throughout history, isthmuses have held immense strategic importance for trade, military operations, and geopolitics. Many of the world's most significant isthmuses have become sites for major canal projects, fundamentally reshaping global commerce and navigation.

Featured Isthmuses

Isthmus of Panama

Connects North and South America, separating the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. Home to the Panama Canal, one of the world's most important shipping routes.

Perekop Isthmus

The narrow land bridge connecting Crimea to mainland Ukraine. A strategic military chokepoint throughout history.

Isthmus of Suez

Connects Africa and Asia, separating the Mediterranean Sea from the Red Sea. Location of the Suez Canal, a vital link in global maritime trade.

Isthmus of Tehuantepec

The narrowest part of Mexico, separating the Gulf of Mexico from the Pacific Ocean. A historically significant trade route and potential alternative to the Panama Canal.
