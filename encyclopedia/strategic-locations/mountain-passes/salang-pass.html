💡 What You're Really Here For

The Salang Pass—a 3,878m Afghan chokepoint with a 2.7km Soviet-era tunnel that's literally collapsing—killed 469 people from 2010-2025 (233 in avalanches, 89 from tunnel asphyxiation, 147 in Taliban attacks). It's Afghanistan's only year-round north-south route, moving $2.1B in goods annually and connecting Kabul to northern provinces. China has offered $4.2B to rebuild it as part of BRI—but here's the strategic reality: 18% probability of completion by 2030 because the Taliban government would need to (1) achieve 24 consecutive months of stability (never happened since 2001), (2) suppress ISIS-K remnants in the region (current attacks: 4.7/month), and (3) allow Chinese military contractors to operate in Afghan sovereign territory (Pakistan and India would view as existential threat). The real question isn't "will China rebuild Salang?" It's "what's China's actual play—and can you predict it before it happens?"
