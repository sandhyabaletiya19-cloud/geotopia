Ablation

Loss of ice from glaciers through melting, evaporation, or calving

+

📖 Explanation

Process where glaciers lose mass through surface melting, sublimation, wind erosion, and calving. Critical indicator of climate change and sea-level rise.

🔑 Key Features

  • Occurs in ablation zone of glaciers
  • Highest during summer months
  • Measured in meters water equivalent
  • Accelerated by dark surfaces absorbing heat

🌍 Examples

🇬🇱 Greenland Ice Sheet

Loses 280 billion tons of ice annually. 2019 single-day melt released 12 billion tons into ocean.

🇨🇭 Aletsch Glacier

Europe's largest glacier lost 3km length since 1870. May lose 90% volume by 2100.

Abrasion

Erosion by rock fragments scraping surfaces like sandpaper

+

📖 Explanation

Mechanical erosion where particles carried by wind, water, or ice grind against rock surfaces. Creates smooth surfaces and distinctive landforms.

🔑 Key Features

  • Requires transporting agent and abrasive material
  • Creates striations and polished surfaces
  • More effective with angular particles
  • Produces fine rock flour sediment

🌍 Examples

🇺🇸 Yosemite Valley

Glacial abrasion carved U-shaped valley over millions of years. Polished granite walls visible today.

🇦🇪 UAE Desert Rocks

Wind-driven sand created mushroom-shaped pedestal rocks over thousands of years.

Abyss

Deep ocean zone from 4,000 to 6,000 meters depth

+

📖 Explanation

Deepest ocean layer with near-freezing temperatures, crushing pressure, and complete darkness. Covers 75% of ocean floor.

🔑 Key Features

  • Depth: 4,000-6,000 meters below surface
  • Temperature: 2-4°C constant
  • Pressure: 400+ atmospheres
  • No sunlight penetration

🌍 Examples

🌊 Mariana Trench

Deepest point on Earth at 11,034m. Only 3 humans have descended to bottom.

🌊 Atlantic Abyss

Contains Titanic wreck at 3,800m depth. Submarine cables cross here.

Acid Rain

Rain with pH below 5.6 from air pollution

+

📖 Explanation

Precipitation contaminated by sulfuric and nitric acids from fossil fuel emissions. Damages ecosystems, buildings, and health.

🔑 Key Features

  • pH below 5.6 (can reach pH 2)
  • Caused by SO₂ and NOₓ emissions
  • Travels 1,000+ km from source
  • Both wet and dry deposition

🌍 Examples

🇩🇪 Black Forest

1980s crisis killed 52% of trees. pH 3.5 rain from industrial emissions.

🇨🇳 Southern China

40% of territory affected. pH 3.0 rain damages $13B crops annually.

Afforestation

Planting forests on previously non-forested land

+

📖 Explanation

Creating new forests on grasslands, deserts, or degraded areas. Key climate change mitigation strategy capturing carbon dioxide.

🔑 Key Features

  • Creates new forest on historically bare land
  • Carbon sequestration: 2-5 tons CO₂/hectare/year
  • 20-30 years to mature forest
  • Best with native species

🌍 Examples

🇨🇳 Great Green Wall

4,500km forest barrier against Gobi Desert. 66 billion trees planted since 1978.

🇪🇹 Ethiopia Record

350 million trees in 12 hours (2019). Goal: 20 billion by 2024.
