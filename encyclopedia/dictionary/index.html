0001
Aa /ˈɑːɑː/
Volcanology
Rough, jagged lava with sharp clinkery surface; Hawaiian origin...
Diagram
Blocky lava flow

Full Definition

Aa (pronounced "ah-ah") is a type of basaltic lava characterized by a rough, jagged, clinkery surface made of broken lava blocks called "clinkers." The term comes from Hawaiian—describing the pain of walking barefoot on this sharp surface. Aa forms when viscous or cooler lava flows slowly, causing the solidifying crust to fracture into angular fragments.

Ablation /əˈbleɪʃən/
Glaciology
Loss of ice/snow from glacier via melting, sublimation, or calving...
Process
Ice loss diagram

Full Definition

Ablation includes all processes removing snow, ice, or water from a glacier: melting (most common), sublimation (ice→vapor), calving (icebergs breaking off), and wind erosion. The ablation zone is the lower glacier where annual loss exceeds accumulation. Climate change has dramatically accelerated ablation worldwide.

Abrasion /əˈbreɪʒən/
Erosion

Full Definition

Abrasion is mechanical rock wearing through friction from particles carried by wind, water, ice, or gravity—like natural sandpaper. Types: glacial (striations), fluvial (river smoothing), aeolian (desert shaping), coastal (wave action).
