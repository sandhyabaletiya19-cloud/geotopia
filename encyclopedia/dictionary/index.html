Term of the Day — March 15, 2026

Ablation

/əˈbleɪʃən/

The combined processes by which a glacier, ice sheet, or snow cover loses mass, including melting, evaporation, sublimation, wind erosion, and calving of icebergs. Ablation is the opposite of accumulation and is a critical factor in determining glacier health and response to climate change.

Geology

Tectonic Plates

/tekˈtɒnɪk pleɪts/

Massive segments of Earth's lithosphere that move, float, and sometimes collide or slide against each other on the semi-fluid asthenosphere beneath.

Greek: tektonikos Learn more
Glaciology

Glacier

/ˈɡlæsiər/

A persistent body of dense ice constantly moving under its own weight, formed where the accumulation of snow exceeds its ablation over many years.

French: glace Learn more
Climatology

Monsoon

/mɒnˈsuːn/

A seasonal reversing wind accompanied by corresponding changes in precipitation, caused by differential heating between land and ocean.

Arabic: mawsim Learn more
Oceanography

Tsunami

/tsuːˈnɑːmi/

A series of ocean waves caused by large-scale displacement of water, typically from earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or underwater landslides.

Japanese: 津波 Learn more
Ecology

Biome

/ˈbaɪoʊm/

A large-scale community of organisms defined by distinctive plant and animal communities that have adapted to a particular climate and environment.

Greek: bios Learn more
Geomorphology

Erosion

/ɪˈroʊʒən/

The process by which rock, soil, and other surface materials are worn away and transported by natural agents such as water, wind, ice, and gravity.

Latin: erodere Learn more
