📋 Key Urban Models

  • Central Place Theory: Settlements organized in hexagonal patterns • Higher-order places serve larger hinterlands
  • Rank-Size Rule: 2nd largest city = 1/2 of largest • 3rd = 1/3 • Creates log-linear distribution
  • Primate City: Single dominant city 2x larger than second • Common in developing nations
  • World System Cities: Alpha++ (NYC, London) → Alpha+ → Alpha → Beta → Gamma classification
  • Urban Influence Zone: Distance decay model • Service availability decreases with distance

Rural Population Trends

📋 Rural Settlement Factors

  • Water Supply: Villages form near rivers, wells, springs • Critical for agriculture & survival
  • Terrain: Flat land preferred • Valley floors vs hilltop settlements for defense
  • Soil Quality: Fertile areas attract nucleated villages • Poor soil = dispersed pattern
  • Climate: Harsh climates = clustered for protection • Mild = dispersed possible
  • Land Tenure: Feudal systems = nucleated • Homesteading = dispersed
  • Transport: Linear patterns along roads, railways, rivers

⚖️ Formal vs Informal Settlements

🏢 FORMAL Settlements

  • Legal land ownership
  • Building permits obtained
  • Access to utilities (water, electricity)
  • Paved roads & drainage
  • Government services available

🏚️ INFORMAL Settlements

  • No legal land tenure
  • Self-built structures
  • Limited/no utilities
  • Overcrowded conditions
  • Vulnerable to eviction
