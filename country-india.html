Location & Boundaries

India is located in South Asia and occupies the greater part of the Indian subcontinent. It lies between 8°4'N to 37°6'N latitude and 68°7'E to 97°25'E longitude. The Tropic of Cancer (23°30'N) passes through the middle of the country.

India is bounded by the Himalayas in the north, the Bay of Bengal in the east, the Arabian Sea in the west, and the Indian Ocean in the south. The country shares its borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.

Extreme Points of India

Northernmost Point: Indira Col in Siachen Glacier (disputed) / Dafdar in POK (undisputed administrative) at 37°6'N

Southernmost Point: Indira Point (Pygmalion Point) in Great Nicobar Island at 6°45'N. On mainland, it is Kanyakumari at 8°4'N

Easternmost Point: Kibithu in Arunachal Pradesh at 97°25'E

Westernmost Point: Ghuar Mota in Gujarat at 68°7'E

Important Geographic Facts

• Standard Meridian of India passes through Mirzapur, UP (82°30'E)

• Total coastline: 7,516.6 km (mainland: 6,100 km)

• Total land border: 15,106.7 km

• India covers 2.4% of world's geographical area

• Time Zone: IST (UTC+5:30)

🏔️ Physical Geography

✅ FREE PREVIEW

Physiographic Divisions

India can be divided into six major physiographic divisions: The Himalayan Mountains, The Northern Plains, The Peninsular Plateau, The Indian Desert, The Coastal Plains, and The Islands. Each region has distinct physical features that influence climate, vegetation, and human activities.

The Himalayan mountain system in the north forms a natural barrier and is the source of major rivers. The vast Indo-Gangetic plains are among the most fertile regions in the world.

1. The Himalayan Mountains

The Himalayas extend from Indus River in the west to Brahmaputra in the east, covering a distance of about 2,400 km. They can be divided into three parallel ranges:

Greater Himalayas (Himadri): Average height 6,000m, permanently snow-covered. Contains Mt. Everest (8,849m), K2 (8,611m), Kanchenjunga (8,586m)

Lesser Himalayas (Himachal): Average height 3,700-4,500m. Contains Pir Panjal, Dhauladhar, Mahabharat ranges. Hill stations like Shimla, Mussoorie, Nainital located here.

Shiwaliks (Outer Himalayas): Average height 900-1,100m. Youngest range, made of unconsolidated sediments. Duns (longitudinal valleys) found here - Dehradun, Patli Dun.

2. The Northern Plains

Formed by alluvial deposits of Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra river systems. Length: 2,400 km, Width: 240-320 km. Divided into Bhabar, Terai, Bhangar, and Khadar regions.

3. The Peninsular Plateau

One of the oldest landmasses, formed of igneous and metamorphic rocks. Includes Central Highlands (Malwa Plateau, Bundelkhand, Baghelkhand) and Deccan Plateau (Karnataka Plateau, Telangana Plateau, etc.)

Drainage Systems of India

India's drainage system consists of rivers that empty into the Bay of Bengal (east-flowing) and those that drain into the Arabian Sea (west-flowing). The Himalayan rivers are perennial and carry water throughout the year, while the peninsular rivers are mostly seasonal.

The Ganga-Brahmaputra system is the largest river system in India. The Ganga originates from Gangotri glacier and traverses 2,525 km before emptying into the Bay of Bengal.

Climate of India - Overview

India has a monsoon type of climate, which is controlled by monsoon winds. The country experiences four main seasons: Winter (Dec-Feb), Summer (Mar-May), Southwest Monsoon (Jun-Sep), and Retreating Monsoon (Oct-Nov).

The climate varies from tropical in the south to temperate in the north. The Himalayas protect India from the cold Central Asian air masses, giving it a warmer climate compared to other regions at similar latitudes.

Seasons of India

Winter Season (December to February): Cold weather period with temperatures ranging from 10-15°C in north to 24-25°C in south. Western disturbances bring rainfall to northwestern India.

Summer Season (March to May): Hot weather period. Temperatures reach 45-48°C in northern plains. Loo (hot, dry winds) blows in north India. Mango showers in Kerala and Karnataka.

Southwest Monsoon (June to September): Accounts for 75-90% of annual rainfall. Arrives in Kerala by June 1, reaches entire country by July 15. Arabian Sea branch and Bay of Bengal branch.

Retreating Monsoon (October to November): Also called Northeast Monsoon. Causes rainfall in Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh. Cyclones form in Bay of Bengal.
