📍 Geographic Location

✅ Free Preview

India is located in South Asia, occupying the greater part of the Indian subcontinent. It lies between 8°4'N to 37°6'N latitude and 68°7'E to 97°25'E longitude.

The country is bounded by the Himalayas in the north and surrounded by the Bay of Bengal, Arabian Sea, and Indian Ocean.

Extreme Points of India

North: Indira Col, Siachen (37°6'N)

South: Indira Point, Great Nicobar (6°45'N)

East: Kibithu, Arunachal Pradesh (97°25'E)

West: Ghuar Mota, Gujarat (68°7'E)

🏔️ Physical Geography

✅ Free Preview

India has six physiographic divisions: Himalayas, Northern Plains, Peninsular Plateau, Indian Desert, Coastal Plains, and Islands.

The Himalayan system forms a natural barrier in the north, while the Indo-Gangetic plains are among the most fertile in the world.

The Himalayan Mountains

Greater Himalayas (Himadri): Average 6,000m, Mt. Everest, K2

Lesser Himalayas (Himachal): Pir Panjal, Dhauladhar ranges

Shiwaliks (Outer): Youngest range, Duns valleys
