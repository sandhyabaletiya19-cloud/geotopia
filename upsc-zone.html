🌍 Physical Geography

Origin of Earth & Universe

The Big Bang Theory

The most widely accepted theory for the origin of the universe:

  • When: Approximately 13.8 billion years ago
  • Initial State: All matter concentrated in a singularity (infinitely small, hot, dense point)
  • Expansion: Universe has been expanding ever since
  • Evidence: Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (CMBR), Red Shift of galaxies
🔑 UPSC Important: Edwin Hubble discovered that galaxies are moving away from each other, providing evidence for an expanding universe. This is known as Hubble's Law.

Formation of Solar System

Theory Proposed By Key Concept
Nebular Hypothesis Kant & Laplace Rotating nebula contracted to form sun and planets
Planetesimal Theory Chamberlin & Moulton Passing star pulled material from sun
Binary Star Theory Russell & Lyttleton Sun had a companion star
Solar Nebular Theory Modern (Accepted) Rotating disk of gas & dust (~4.6 billion years ago)

⚡ Frequently Asked in UPSC:

• Earth's age determined by: Radiometric dating

Interior of Earth

Earth's Layers

Layer Depth (km) Density (g/cm³) Composition
Crust 0-35 (Cont.), 0-5 (Oceanic) 2.7-3.0 Silica, Alumina (SIAL)
Mantle 35-2,900 3.4-5.6 Silica, Magnesium (SIMA)
Outer Core 2,900-5,100 9.9-12.2 Iron, Nickel (Liquid)
Inner Core 5,100-6,371 12.8-13.1 Iron, Nickel (Solid - NIFE)

Discontinuities

  • Conrad Discontinuity: Between Upper (SIAL) and Lower (SIMA) Crust
  • Mohorovičić (Moho): Between Crust and Mantle (35 km depth)
  • Repetti Discontinuity: Between Upper and Lower Mantle
  • Gutenberg Discontinuity: Between Mantle and Core (2,900 km)
  • Lehmann Discontinuity: Between Outer and Inner Core (5,100 km)
📝 Mnemonic: "Can Men Really Grow Legs" = Conrad, Moho, Repetti, Gutenberg, Lehmann (top to bottom)

Seismic Waves

Wave Type Nature Speed Medium
P-Waves (Primary) Longitudinal (Push-Pull) Fastest (5-8 km/s) Solid, Liquid, Gas
S-Waves (Secondary) Transverse (Side-to-side) Slower (3-5 km/s) Solid Only
L-Waves (Surface) Complex Slowest Surface - Most destructive
🔑 UPSC Important: S-waves cannot pass through liquid outer core. This is the key evidence that the outer core is liquid!

Shadow Zones

  • P-Wave Shadow Zone: 103° to 142° from epicenter
  • S-Wave Shadow Zone: Beyond 103° from epicenter (cannot pass through liquid core)

Plate Tectonics

Theory Development

1912
Continental Drift Theory - Alfred Wegener proposed that continents were once joined as Pangaea
1960s
Sea Floor Spreading - Harry Hess explained mid-ocean ridge formation
1967
Plate Tectonics Theory - McKenzie, Parker, and Morgan combined all evidence

Earthquakes

Key Terms

  • Focus (Hypocenter): Point of origin of earthquake inside Earth
  • Epicenter: Point on surface directly above focus
  • Seismograph: Instrument to record seismic waves
  • Seismology: Study of earthquakes

Earthquake Measurement

Scale Measures Range
Richter Scale Magnitude (Energy released) 1-10 (Logarithmic)
Mercalli Scale Intensity (Damage/Effects) I-XII (Roman numerals)
Moment Magnitude Modern, more accurate Used for large earthquakes
📝 Remember: Each unit increase in Richter scale = 10x increase in amplitude, 31.6x increase in energy!

Types by Activity

Type Definition Examples

🔑 India's Volcanoes:
• Barren Island: Only active volcano in India (Andaman)
• Narcondam Island: Dormant volcano (Andaman)
• Dhinodhar Hills: Extinct (Gujarat)
• Deccan Traps: Volcanic origin (65 million years ago)

Types by Shape

Type Characteristics Examples
Shield Volcano Broad, gentle slopes; fluid lava Mauna Loa (Hawaii)
Composite/Stratovolcano Steep, symmetrical; lava & ash layers Mt. Fuji, Mt. Vesuvius
Cinder Cone Small, steep; built from particles Parícutin (Mexico)
Caldera Large depression; collapsed crater Yellowstone, Crater Lake

Global Distribution

  • Ring of Fire: Pacific Ocean margins (75% of world's volcanoes)
  • Mid-Atlantic Ridge: Iceland, Azores
  • East African Rift: Mt. Kilimanjaro, Mt. Kenya
  • Mediterranean Belt: Mt. Vesuvius, Mt. Etna
⚡ Volcanic Landforms:
• Intrusive: Batholith, Laccolith, Sill, Dyke
• Extrusive: Lava Plateau (Deccan Traps), Volcanic Mountain

Practice MCQs - Physical Geography

Test Your Knowledge
1. Which discontinuity separates the Earth's crust from the mantle?
A Gutenberg Discontinuity
B Mohorovičić Discontinuity
C Lehmann Discontinuity
D Conrad Discontinuity
Answer: B
Mohorovičić (Moho) Discontinuity separates the crust from the mantle at about 35 km depth. It was discovered by Andrija Mohorovičić in 1909.
2. Which type of seismic waves cannot travel through liquid?
A P-Waves
B S-Waves
C L-Waves
D All of the above
Answer: B
S-Waves (Secondary waves) are transverse waves and cannot travel through liquids. This property helped scientists determine that Earth's outer core is liquid.
3. The only active volcano in India is located in:
A Barren Island
B Narcondam Island
C Dhinodhar Hills
D Car Nicobar
Answer: A
Barren Island in Andaman & Nicobar Islands is the only confirmed active volcano in India. It last erupted in 2017.
4. Which of the following plates is entirely oceanic?
A Pacific Plate
B Indo-Australian Plate
C African Plate
D Eurasian Plate
Answer: A
Pacific Plate is the largest tectonic plate and is almost entirely oceanic. It's the only major plate that is predominantly oceanic.
5. The age of the Earth is approximately:
A 3.8 billion years
B 4.0 billion years
C 4.6 billion years
D 13.8 billion years
Answer: C
Earth is approximately 4.6 billion years old. 13.8 billion years is the age of the Universe. This was determined through radiometric dating of rocks and meteorites.
