Chapter 3: Plate Tectonics

3.1 Continental Drift Theory

Alfred Wegener (1912): All continents were joined as Pangaea surrounded by Panthalassa ocean, began breaking ~200 MYA.

Evidence:

TypeEvidence
Jigsaw FitS. America & Africa coastlines match
FossilsMesosaurus in both S. America & Africa
GeologicalRock formations match across continents
GlacialGlacial deposits in tropical areas

3.2 Sea Floor Spreading

Harry Hess (1960s): New oceanic crust created at mid-ocean ridges and spreads outward.

Evidence: Magnetic stripes on ocean floor, rocks youngest near ridges.

9.3 Temperature Distribution

Horizontal Distribution:

  • Temperature decreases from equator to poles
  • Isotherms run parallel to latitudes (generally)
  • Deviation over land masses (continental effect)

Vertical Distribution:

  • Normal Lapse Rate: 6.5°C decrease per 1000m
  • Temperature Inversion: Temperature increases with height (opposite of normal)
🔑 Temperature Inversion: Causes fog, smog, frost; common in valleys during winter nights.

9.5 Clouds - Types & Formation

Cloud: Visible mass of water droplets or ice crystals suspended in atmosphere.

Cloud Formation Process:

  1. Air rises (convection, orographic, frontal lifting)
  2. Air expands due to lower pressure
  3. Temperature drops (adiabatic cooling)
  4. Reaches dew point → condensation begins
  5. Water vapor condenses around condensation nuclei (dust, salt, pollen)
  6. Cloud forms

Cloud Classification by Height:

CategoryHeightCloud Types
High Clouds6-12 kmCirrus, Cirrostratus, Cirrocumulus
Middle Clouds2-6 kmAltostratus, Altocumulus
Low Clouds0-2 kmStratus, Stratocumulus, Nimbostratus
Vertical DevelopmentVariableCumulus, Cumulonimbus

📝 Naming Logic:
• Cirro- = High clouds (ice crystals)
• Alto- = Middle clouds
• Strato-/Stratus = Layered, sheet-like
• Cumulo-/Cumulus = Puffy, heaped
• Nimbo-/Nimbus = Rain-bearing

UPSC Pattern Questions
1. Which discontinuity separates crust from mantle?
A Gutenberg
B Mohorovičić
C Lehmann
D Conrad
Answer: B - Moho at ~35 km separates crust from mantle.
2. Which clouds are associated with thunderstorms?
A Cirrus
B Stratus
C Altostratus
D Cumulonimbus
Answer: D - Cumulonimbus are towering clouds causing thunderstorms, heavy rain, hail, lightning.
3. Barchan dunes have horns pointing:
A Upwind
B Downwind
C No direction
D Perpendicular
Answer: B - Barchan horns point downwind. Parabolic point upwind.
4. El Niño causes in India:
A Weak monsoon, drought
B Strong monsoon, floods
C No effect
D Earthquakes
Answer: A - El Niño (warm Pacific) causes weak monsoon & drought risk in India.
5. Spring tides occur during:
A New & Full Moon
B First & Third Quarter
C Spring season
D Summer solstice
Answer: A - Spring tides (highest) occur when Sun-Earth-Moon are aligned.
6. India's only active volcano is:
A Barren Island
B Narcondam
C Dhinodhar
D Deccan Traps
Answer: A - Barren Island (Andaman) is India's only active volcano.
7. Ozone layer is in which atmospheric layer?
A Troposphere
B Stratosphere
C Mesosphere
D Thermosphere
Answer: B - Stratosphere (12-50 km) contains ozone layer at 15-35 km.
8. Limestone metamorphoses into:
A Slate
B Marble
C Quartzite
D Gneiss
Answer: B - Limestone → Marble. Sandstone → Quartzite. Shale → Slate.

Formed by deposition of alluvium by Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra rivers. Length: ~2,400 km | Width: 150-300 km | Area: ~7 lakh km²

ZoneLocationCharacteristics

3.4 Importance

  • Most fertile region (alluvial soil)
  • Food bowl of India (rice, wheat, sugarcane)
  • Most densely populated
  • Rich groundwater

Chapter 4: The Peninsular Plateau

4.1 Characteristics

  • One of oldest landmasses (part of Gondwanaland)
  • Average height: 600-900 m
  • Triangular shape, slopes eastward
  • Bounded by Western & Eastern Ghats

5.2 Ganga System

  • Headstreams: Bhagirathi + Alaknanda = Ganga (at Devprayag)
  • Left Bank: Ramganga, Gomti, Ghaghara, Gandak, Kosi, Mahananda
  • Right Bank: Yamuna (largest), Son
  • Delta: Sundarbans (world's largest mangrove)

5.3 Brahmaputra System

  • Names: Tsangpo (Tibet) → Siang/Dihang (Arunachal) → Brahmaputra (Assam) → Jamuna (Bangladesh)
  • Tributaries: Subansiri, Kameng, Manas, Teesta
  • Features: Braided river in Assam; Majuli (largest river island)

5.4 West-Flowing Rivers

Why Narmada & Tapti flow west? They flow through rift valleys (linear depressions).

  • Narmada, Tapti, Mahi, Sabarmati, Luni
  • Form estuaries (not deltas)

Chapter 6: Climate of India

6.1 Factors Affecting Climate

  • Latitude: Tropic of Cancer divides India
  • Himalayas: Block cold Central Asian winds; trap monsoon
  • Distance from Sea: Continental vs maritime
  • Altitude: 6.5°C decrease per 1000m
  • Monsoon Winds: Determine rainfall

6.2 Seasons

SeasonMonthsFeatures
WinterDec-FebNE Monsoon; Western Disturbances bring rain to N. India
SummerMar-MayLoo (hot winds); Nor'westers; dust storms
MonsoonJun-SepSW Monsoon; 75% of annual rainfall
Post-MonsoonOct-NovRetreating monsoon; Bay of Bengal cyclones

6.3 Southwest Monsoon

  • Onset: Kerala coast around June 1 (±7 days)
  • Arabian Sea Branch: Hits Western Ghats first
  • Bay of Bengal Branch: Brings rain to NE, deflected by Himalayas
  • Withdrawal: September (starts from NW India)
